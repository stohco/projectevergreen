# State of the World at the Edge of Canon — Renegade Immortal

> **The foundational framing document for Wang Lin's branch.**
>
> This document replaces the earlier (incorrect) "post-Wang-Lin world" framing.
> The player does NOT enter a world "after" Wang Lin. The player enters Wang
> Lin's branch **at the edge of recorded canon** — a world permanently shaped
> by Wang Lin's history, while Wang Lin himself still exists.

---

## Part 0 — The Reframe (Why This Document Exists)

### The wrong framing (corrected)

The earlier architecture said:

> *"The player enters a post-Wang-Lin world."*

This is wrong. It sounds similar to the correct framing, but it is fundamentally different.

### The correct framing

> **"The player enters Wang Lin's branch at the edge of recorded canon."**

What this means, concretely:

1. **Wang Lin is NOT gone.** His true body exists on the Immortal Astral Continent. Canonical time has paused there. He is not dead; he is not ascended-away; he is not a memory. He is present, but canonical time has frozen at the moment of his Transcendence-with-Li-Muwan.

2. **Wang Lin's manifestation still journeys.** A manifestation of Wang Lin travels through the lower worlds. The player has already been traveling with this manifestation (per the cross-novel architectural note: Wang Lin's manifestation returns to his home world in AWWP). The manifestation is a companion, not a quest-giver.

3. **The world is permanently shaped by Wang Lin's history.** Every place the player visits has been touched by what Wang Lin did. Ruined sects. Abandoned inheritances. Altered spirit veins. Political vacuums. Terrified surviving factions. Forgotten battlefields. These are not "post-Wang-Lin" — they are the **current state of a world where Wang Lin's journey already happened**.

4. **Canonical time will resume when the player reaches that tier.** The player is an ADDITIONAL protagonist, not Wang Lin's replacement. When the player reaches the IAC / Heaven Trampling tier, canonical time resumes and Wang Lin continues living. The player and Wang Lin coexist.

### Why this matters for design

The wrong framing leads to two bad design patterns:

- **Bad Pattern A — "Replacement antagonists":** inventing new Teng Huayu-tier or All-Seer-tier NPCs to replace the ones Wang Lin killed. This contradicts canon (the antagonists are dead) AND misses the point (the threat is the universe, not a villain).

- **Bad Pattern B — "Replaying Wang Lin's plot":** sending the player through Wang Lin's story beats (find the bead, join Heng Yue, fight the Teng Clan, etc.). This is fanfiction, not a living world.

The correct framing leads to:

- **Good Pattern — "Living in the consequences":** the player explores the consequences of Wang Lin's journey while forging their own path. The Teng Clan is gone (Wang Lin exterminated them) — but the political vacuum they left is real. New sects rose on their ruins. New cultivators were born. The world moved on. The player lives in THAT world.

## Part 1 — The Threat Model (Corrected)

### The wrong question

The earlier architecture asked: *"What threats does the player face?"* and then tried to answer it by inventing replacement NPCs.

### The right answer

**The universe.**

The player doesn't need a replacement Teng Huayuan. The actual state of the world — after Wang Lin's journey — is already enough to create centuries of conflict.

### What Wang Lin left behind

Wang Lin's journey (108 canon events across ~2000 years) left the Cave World transformed:

| Category | Examples | Threat type |
|---|---|---|
| **Abandoned inheritances** | Tu Si's Ancient God legacy (partially claimed); Bai Fan's Collection Pavilion (raided); Soul Refining Sect inheritance (claimed by Wang Lin); Restriction Mountain trial (completed) | Dangerous to claim — residual restrictions, guardian beasts, rival treasure hunters |
| **Ruined sects** | Teng Clan (exterminated); Dao Demon Sect (master devoured); Dao Devil Sect (annihilated); Dong Lin Sect (destroyed); Rank 9 God Sect (master slain) | Political vacuums — power scrambles, territorial disputes |

| **Political vacuums** | Heavenly Fate Sect (masterless after All-Seer's death); Water Spirit Sect (masterless after Daoist Water's death); Blood Planet (ruin after Blood Ancestor's death) | Power realignment — smaller sects expand, new alliances form |
| **Destroyed civilizations** | Multiple planets in Allheaven Star System (Wang Lin destroyed them during the Yao Family kill-order arc); Luo Tian Thunder Immortal Realm (collapsed before Wang Lin's era) | Lost knowledge, reclaiming beasts, void hazards |

### Plus: new generations

Cultivation never stopped. Thousands of cultivators continue being born every generation. Some become geniuses. Some become tyrants. Some become heroes. Some become monsters. Some become sect leaders.

**These are NOT replacements for canonical villains.** They are simply the next generation. That is exactly how history works.

A cultivator born 200 years after Wang Lin dissolved the Realm-Sealing Grand Array is not "the new All-Seer." They are a person who grew up in a world where the seal is gone, where Third-Step cultivation is possible again, where the political map has been redrawn. Their goals are their own.

### The real conflict

> **"How does a civilization rebuild after Wang Lin changed the world?"**

That is the central question of the player's experience. Not "who replaces Wang Lin's enemies" — but "what does the world look like when the most powerful cultivator in 100,000 years has finished his journey and paused at the edge of canon?"

## Part 2 — The Manifestation Companion

### Canon basis

Per the cross-novel architectural note (CANON_RI_CHARACTER_DECISIONS.md CD-02): Wang Lin's manifestation returns to his home world. His true body remains at the edge of canon (on the IAC, having Transcended with Li Muwan). The manifestation is the aspect of Wang Lin that exists in the lower worlds.

### The companion mechanic

The player has **already been traveling with Wang Lin's manifestation** before the game begins. This is not a quest-giver relationship. It is a companion relationship.

The manifestation:

- **Does NOT give quests.** Wang Lin is not interested in directing the player's life. He has already lived his.
- **Does NOT fight for the player.** The manifestation is a witness, not a bodyguard.
- **Comments on significant places.** When the player visits a place important to Wang Lin's journey, the manifestation may comment. Example: *"This valley used to be different."* This makes the world feel lived-in.
- **Reveals history gradually.** The manifestation's comments are the primary vehicle for delivering historical context. The player learns what happened here by being here with the manifestation.
- **Has its own emotional state.** The manifestation is Wang Lin's remnant will. It has feelings about the places it visits. Some places make it sorrowful (Wang Family Village, where his parents were killed). Some make it reflective (the mortal village where he lived as a mortal). Some make it wary (the Ancient Immortal Domain, where he killed the Seven-Colored Daoist).

### What the manifestation knows

The manifestation carries Wang Lin's memories up to the edge of canon. It knows:

- Everything Wang Lin experienced (all 108 timeline events)
- The locations of his abandoned inheritances (but will not hand them over — the player must find their own path)
- The fate of every faction he destroyed or weakened
- The nature of the cosmology (the nested-sealed structure, the Joss Flame harvest, the Cave World ownership)

### What the manifestation does NOT do

- Does NOT hand the player the Heaven-Defying Bead (it is fused with Wang Lin's primordial spirit)
- Does NOT transmit Wang Lin's techniques (the player must learn their own dao; the manifestation may hint but will not teach)
- Does NOT solve the player's problems (the player is the protagonist of their own story)
- Does NOT leave (the manifestation is a permanent companion; it chose to travel with the player)

### Design intent

The manifestation companion is the bridge between "Wang Lin's story" and "the player's story." It ensures the world feels like Wang Lin's world (he is present, his history is real) without making the player replay Wang Lin's story (the player forges their own path, with Wang Lin as a witness).

### Chain 1 — The Cave World's Creation (E02-E05)

**Event:** The Seven-Colored Daoist creates the Cave World (~−100,000 BW), creates the Three Souls and Seven Spirits (E03), bestows the Heaven-Defying Pearl to the Realm-Sealing Supreme (E04), and places the Realm-Sealing Grand Array (E05).

**Immediate effects:**
- The Cave World exists as an artificial pocket-dimension farm.
- Mortal populations inside begin generating Joss Flames through worship.
- The Realm-Sealing Grand Array suppresses Third-Step cultivation in the Sealed Realm (inner half).
- The Three Souls and Seven Spirits (Situ Nan, Qing Shui, Tan Lang, Xie Qing, etc.) are dispersed as fragments of the Daoist.

**100-year effects:**
- The Joss Flame harvest loop stabilizes. The Daoist's power grows.
- Cultivators inside the Cave World hit the seal's ceiling (Nirvana Fruit / Heaven Blight) and cannot advance. A generation of stuck quasi-Third-Step cultivators forms the "Heaven Blight" tier.
- The Three Souls and Seven Spirits begin their reincarnation cycles.

**1000-year effects:**
- The Cave World's cultivation civilization matures under the seal. Sects form, rise, and fall — all within the ceiling.
- The Outer Realm (unsealed half) develops a different cultivation culture: Third-Step cultivators CAN arise there, but they are hunted by the Daoist's agents if they threaten the farm.
- The Heaven-Defying Pearl passes through the Realm-Sealing Supreme's lineage, eventually sent back through time to Wang Lin.

**Current world state (edge of canon):**
- The Seven-Colored Daoist is DEAD (killed by Wang Lin, E30). The Cave World is now "Wang Lin's Cave World."
- The Realm-Sealing Grand Array is DISSOLVED (Wang Lin dissolved it after becoming owner). The Unnamed Wheel Formation (E70) replaced it, then Wang Lin dissolved that too when he became owner.
- **The seal is gone.** Third-Step cultivators can now arise naturally in the Sealed Realm for the first time in ~100,000 years. This is a cosmological event — the cultivation world is reorganizing around the new reality.
- The Joss Flame harvest loop belongs to Wang Lin. He does NOT harvest — he releases. The mortal populations inside the Cave World no longer generate Joss Flames for an owner; they generate them for themselves (a slow cultivation renaissance).
- The Three Souls and Seven Spirits are mostly dispersed. Situ Nan (Green Soul) is with Wang Lin. Qing Shui (Slaughter Soul) was consumed by Wang Lin. Tan Lang (Yellow Soul) was reduced to a pet. Xie Qing (Third Soul) ended his own life and entrusted his memories to Wang Lin.
- **Player implication:** The player enters a Cave World where the seal is gone, the owner is benevolent (Wang Lin), and the cultivation ceiling has been lifted. This is an unprecedented era of opportunity — and danger, because the power vacuum at the top means ambitious cultivators are racing for Third-Step.

### Chain 2 — The Tu Si Ancient God Legacy (E06)

**Event:** Tu Si, an Ancient God-tier cultivator (~−50,000 BW), leaves behind a memory legacy inside the Land of the Ancient God, including the Ancient God Tactic.

**Immediate effects:**
- The Land of the Ancient God becomes a high-tier ruin containing Tu Si's body and the Restriction Mountain trial.
- The Ancient God Tactic (body-cultivation art whose essence is "plunder everything") is sealed inside, awaiting an inheritor.

**100-year effects:**
- Word of the Ancient God ruin spreads. Treasure hunters attempt it. Most die. The ruin's reputation as a death zone solidifies.

**1000-year effects:**
- The Land of the Ancient God becomes a legend. Most cultivators treat it as myth. A few powerful sects keep records of its location but do not attempt it (too dangerous).

**Current world state:**
- Wang Lin inherited Tu Si's legacy (E17, Ch. 190-199). His hair turned white. He reconstructed his body as a 1-Star Ancient God.
- **The Land of the Ancient God is partially depleted.** Wang Lin claimed the core inheritance (the Ancient God Tactic, the Restriction Flag refining method). But the ruin itself still exists — the Restriction Mountain trial (E43) still stands, and Wang Lin was only the 4th person in history to complete it.
- **Player implication:** The player can still attempt the Restriction Mountain trial. It is harder now (Tu Si's core legacy is gone), but residual restrictions and the trial's structure remain. The player can learn a *partial* Ancient God body-cultivation track — not Wang Lin's full inheritance, but the foundation. This is one path to body-cultivation; the player can also find other Ancient God ruins (Tu Si was not the only Ancient God in history).

### Chain 4 — The Wang Family Village Massacre (E13)

**Event:** Teng Huayuan (Teng Clan patriarch) slaughters the Wang family (~Year 18-20) to take revenge on Wang Lin. Wang Lin's parents are killed; their souls trapped in Teng Huayuan's soul flag.

- The Wang Family Village and the Teng Clan territory are both ruins. Nature reclaims them. Spirit beasts migrate in. The local spirit veins, untouched for centuries, accumulate energy — making the ruins unexpectedly rich cultivation sites.

- **The Wang Family Village is a pilgrimage site / haunted ruin.** The manifestation will comment here: *"This is where I was born. They called me Tie Zhu."* The site has accumulated ~2000 years of spiritual energy (undisturbed spirit vein). They know the story: "The old Teng Clan fell because they angered a heaven-defying cultivator." Some admire Wang Lin. Some fear him. Some don't believe the stories.
- **Player implication:** Both sites are explorable. The Wang Family Village offers high-quality cultivation but heart-demon risk. The Teng Clan ruins offer scavenging opportunities and a minor sect interaction. Neither site is "Wang Lin's quest" — they are places with history.

## Part 5 — The Decision Horizon Taxonomy

> **Per user directive:** "Instead of Goals/Needs/Resources, add Decision Horizon.
> Some NPCs think minutes. Others years. Others millennia. The Seven-Colored
> Daoist doesn't make decisions like Teng Huayuan. He thinks on cosmic timescales.
> That changes planning completely."

The Decision Horizon is added to the NPC planner data (CANON_RI_CHARACTER_DECISIONS.md). It classifies how far ahead an NPC plans and how patient they are.

### The 6 horizons

| Horizon | Timescale | Description | Example NPCs |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MINUTES** | Seconds to hours | Reactive. Acts on immediate stimuli. No long-term planning. Combat reflexes, survival instinct. | Most beasts; low-realm cultivators in combat; panicked NPCs |
| **DAYS** | Hours to weeks | Tactical. Plans within an arc. Considers immediate consequences but not generational effects. | Teng Huayuan (clan-scale scheming); Yao Xixue (ambush planning); most sect elders |
| **YEARS** | Months to decades | Strategic. Plans across a cultivation phase. Considers sect-level consequences. | The All-Seer (possession plot spans decades); Heng Yue Sect master; most mid-tier faction leaders |
| **DECADES** | Decades to centuries | Generational. Plans across multiple mortal generations. Considers regional consequences. | Daoist Water (sect defense across generations); high-tier sect patriarchs; the Blood Ancestor |
| **CENTURIES** | Centuries to millennia | Civilizational. Plans across entire cultivation eras. Considers world-level consequences. | Xie Qing (800-year meditation); the Seal Sovereign; high-tier IAC cultivators |
| **MILLENNIA** | Millennia to cosmic timescales | Cosmic. Plans on the scale of the cosmology itself. Considers reality-level consequences. | The Seven-Colored Daoist (100,000-year farm); Wang Lin (2000-year revival pursuit); Transcendent-tier NPCs |

### Why this matters

The Decision Horizon determines:

1. **Reaction speed.** A MINUTES-horizon NPC reacts instantly to threats. A MILLENNIA-horizon NPC may not react to a threat for decades (it is beneath their notice).

2. **Plotting depth.** A DAYS-horizon NPC plots ambushes. A DECADES-horizon NPC plots sect conquest. A MILLENNIA-horizon NPC plots cosmological shifts.

3. **Patience.** A MINUTES-horizon NPC cannot wait. A MILLENNIA-horizon NPC can wait 10,000 years for the right moment.

4. **Threat assessment.** A MINUTES-horizon NPC assesses threats by immediate danger. A MILLENNIA-horizon NPC assesses threats by whether they threaten the cosmological order.

5. **Player interaction.** The player can surprise a MINUTES-horizon NPC with a days-long scheme. The player CANNOT surprise a MILLENNIA-horizon NPC with anything less than a centuries-long plot.

### Application to edge-of-canon NPCs

At the edge of canon, most canonical antagonists are dead. The NPCs the player encounters are:

- **New-generation cultivators** (DAYS to YEARS horizon) — the next generation, pursuing their own goals
- **Surviving canonical figures** (Xi Zifeng, Ta Shan — DECADES horizon) — Wang Lin's disciples and allies, with long perspectives
- **Wang Lin's manifestation** (MILLENNIA horizon) — a cosmic-tier companion who thinks on transcendence timescales
- **Residual threats** (ruin guardians, beast lords — MINUTES to DAYS) — the dangers of the transformed world

The player themselves starts at MINUTES (a mortal) and grows toward MILLENNIA (as they cultivate). The player's Decision Horizon expands with their realm — a mortal cannot plan in centuries; a Third-Step cultivator can.
