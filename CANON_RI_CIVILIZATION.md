## Part 0 — Civilization Axioms

### Axiom C1: The Joss Flame Economy Underlies All Civilization
Every mortal in the Cave World generates Joss Flame through worship, prayer, and cultural veneration. The Realm-Sealing Grand Array siphons ~30% of this to the Seven-Colored Daoist (the Cave World's owner). Sects, nations, and clans are all — knowingly or unknowingly — Joss Flame farms embedded within a cosmic livestock operation. A sect's "righteousness" or "demonic" alignment is irrelevant to this structural truth. **[Type A, conf 5]**

### Axiom C2: Sect Power = Cultivation Ceiling × Disciple Count × Resource Control
A sect's strength derives from three multiplicative factors: (a) the peak cultivation realm achievable within its territory (itself a function of local spirit vein quality and density, per Ecology Axiom G3), (b) the number of cultivators at each realm tier, and (c) the quantity and quality of controlled resources (spirit stones, herbs, beasts, artifacts, secret realms). A small sect with one Soul Formation elder can dominate a region where no other sect exceeds Core Formation, but would be irrelevant in a region where Nirvana Scryer cultivators are common. **[Type B, conf 4]**

### Axiom C3: Hierarchy Mirrors Cultivation Realms
In canon, every attested sect follows a hierarchical structure that maps onto the cultivation realm ladder. The exact titles vary (Patriarch / Ancestor / Sect Master / Divine Emperor), but the pattern is universal: the strongest cultivator leads, with power delegating downward through realm tiers. "Core disciple" correlates with Foundation Establishment–Core Formation. "Outer disciple" correlates with Qi Condensation. Mortal servants handle logistics. This is not a generic fantasy convention — it is the logical consequence of a society where personal power IS political power. **[Type B, conf 4]**

### Axiom C4: Internal Divisions Reflect Specialization, Not Politics
Where canon attests internal subdivisions (Heavenly Fate Sect's 7 color divisions, Vermilion Bird's generational succession), these reflect functional specialization — different cultivation paths, element affinities, or inheritance lines — rather than political factions. Civil wars within sects are rare and cataclysmic (the 3rd-Gen Vermilion Bird's betrayal being the prime example). **[Type A, conf 5]**

### Axiom C5: Economy Flows Up the Realm Ladder
Resources flow upward: mortals gather herbs → outer disciples process them → inner disciples use pills for breakthrough → core disciples control spirit veins → elders manage trade with other powers → the patriarch manages strategic relationships. Spirit stones and celestial jades are the universal currency. Pills, artifacts, and beast cores are the commodity layer. The Joss Flame economy operates invisibly above all of this. **[Type C, conf 3]**

### Axiom C6: Sect Destruction Creates Ruin Ecology
When a sect is destroyed (Teng Clan, Dao Devil Sect, Dong Lin Sect, Fighting Evil Sect leadership), the ruins follow a predictable decay pattern per Ecology Global Law G4: resentful spirits linger at the tier of the destroyed cultivators, artifacts scatter or get buried, surviving disciples flee to other sects (carrying techniques and grudges), and the local ecology reclaims the territory. These ruins become exploration sites for future cultivators. **[Type B, conf 4]**

## Part 1 — Methodology

### 1.1 Classification System

Every civilization entry is tagged:
- **[A]** = Direct Canon (CANON_CONCRETE, conf 5) — explicitly stated in the novels or wiki
- **[B]** = Canonical Implication (CANON_IMPLIED, conf 4) — must logically exist given canon facts
- **[C]** = Reasonable Reconstruction (REASONABLE_RECONSTRUCTION, conf 3) — likely true, derived from cultivation systems and ecology
- **[D]** = Speculation (SPECULATION, conf 1-2) — possible but unverified; never overwrites canon
- **[F]** = FORBIDDEN — contradicts known canon or user corrections

### 1.2 Structure Per Faction

Each of the 18 fully-detailed factions includes:
1. **Identity** — name, type, alignment, peak realm, headquarters, specialization
2. **Hierarchy** — Patriarch/Leader → Elders → Core Disciples → Inner Disciples → Outer Disciples → Servants → Mortals, with canon-named members at each rank
3. **Economy** — currency, pill production, material sources, trade relationships
4. **Relationships** — allies, enemies, vassals, historical grudges
5. **Internal Divisions** — sub-branches, color divisions, generational lines
6. **Canon Events** — major events involving this faction
7. **Player Encounter Layer** — how the mod should present this faction

### 1.3 Canon-Silent Entities (FORBIDDEN)

The following entity names are NOT attested in canon and MUST NOT appear as canon in the mod:
- "Ten Thousand Demons Sect" → Use Sky Demon Country + Ancient Demon City [F]
- "Xue Yue" → Use Snow Domain Country (雪域国) [F]
- "Heavenly Demon City" → Use Ancient Demon City (古魔城) [F]
- "Suzaku Continent" → Planet Suzaku (朱雀星) is a PLANET, not a continent [F]
- "Heavenly Fate Continent" → Planet Tian Yun (天运星) is a PLANET, not a continent [F]
- "Ancient Emperor's inheritance" → Use Immortal Monarch's Cave Mansion [F]

## Part 6 — Sect Lifecycle Model

### 6.1 Formation (Type C, conf 3)
A new sect forms when:
1. A cultivator reaches a realm significantly higher than the local baseline
2. They attract disciples by offering protection and teachings
3. They claim a spirit vein or territory
4. They establish a reputation (righteous, demonic, or neutral)
Example: Wang Lin's Soul Refining Tribe formed when he taught Soul Refining heritage to the Mountain Valley Tribe.

### 6.2 Growth (Type C, conf 3)
A sect grows by:
1. Producing higher-realm disciples (increasing the peak realm ceiling)
2. Claiming more spirit veins and territory
3. Establishing trade relationships
4. Building alliances or vassal relationships with weaker factions
5. Accumulating Joss Flame through mortal population control

### 6.3 Decline (Type C, conf 3)
A sect declines by:
1. Death or departure of the patriarch/strongest cultivator
2. Loss of spirit veins to rivals or natural depletion
3. Internal power struggles
4. External attack (Teng Clan, Fighting Evil Sect, Dao Devil Sect)
5. Loss of disciple talent pipeline

### 6.4 Destruction (Type A/B, conf 4-5)
Canon-attested destruction patterns:
- **Total Annihilation**: Dao Devil Sect (Wang Lin reversed the Green Devil sacrifice ritual and annihilated them) **[A]**
- **Leadership Assassination + Collapse**: Fighting Evil Sect (Wang Lin killed the leader, released the sect) **[A]**
- **Clan Extermination**: Teng Clan (Wang Lin killed all 9 Nascent Souls + patriarch) **[A]**; Wang Clan (Teng Huayuan's massacre) **[A]**
- **Pre-Arrival Destruction**: Dong Lin Sect (destroyed before Wang Lin arrived) **[A]**

### 6.5 Heritage Transfer (Type A, conf 5)
Canon-attested heritage transfer paths:
1. **Soul Refining Sect → Wang Lin → Soul Refining Tribe** (technique + banner) **[A]**
2. **Vermilion Bird Line** (generational succession, 1st → 2nd → ... → 6th/Wang Lin → 15th/Zhou Wutai) **[A]**
3. **Great Soul Sect → Wang Lin** (Ghostly Sail → Restriction Essence bloodlines) **[A]**
