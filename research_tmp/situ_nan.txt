Get to know Sikong Nan in one minute(from:秒懂百科) 00:59 Si Tu Nan, also known as Si Tu, is a male character in the xianxia novel Renegade Immortal authored by Er Gen . He first appears in Chapter 46, &quot;Old Monster,&quot; of Volume 1, &quot;The Mediocre Youth,&quot; of Renegade Immortal, serving as Wang Lin &#x27;s guide on the path of cultivation. In derivative works, this character is voiced by Wentao Chang , among others. 1 Si Tu Nan possesses a head of red hair, and his face is as sharp as if carved by a knife, with distinct edges and corners, giving an impression of determination and ruggedness. His avatar form differs significantly from his true form, featuring silver hair and crimson eyes, with a flamboyant hairstyle. Si Tu Nan&#x27;s personality is wildly arrogant and unrestrained; his words are extreme, and he tramples on rules. He values loyalty and righteousness deeply, keeping promises to friends, being fiercely protective of his disciple, and possessing a chivalrous spirit. He harbors worldly attachments, pursuing pleasure and power. He is humorous and casual, adept at using slang and self-deprecation. In terms of abilities, he possesses monstrous talent, cultivates the Yellow Spring Ascending Aperture Art, created killing techniques like the &quot;Extinction Finger,&quot; controls the Heaven Defying Pearl, and possesses Samsara Power, Heavenly Eye, among others, with flexible tactics. Si Tu Nan is the reincarnation of the Green Soul from the Three Souls and Seven Spirits of the Seven-Colored Immortal Venerable, endowed with exceptional talent and rapid cultivation speed. He once served as the Second Generation Vermilion Bird Master of the Vermilion Bird Country, elevating it to a sixth-level cultivation nation with his formidable strength. He bore the responsibility of guarding the Vermilion Bird Star for a millennium and successfully sealed the Cultivation Star Crystal. Later, he was severely injured in an ambush by the Third Generation Vermilion Bird Master and Tan Lang , among others, causing his primordial spirit to flee into the Heaven Defying Pearl and fall into a deep sleep. After encountering Wang Lin by chance, he gave up the chance to seize Wang Lin&#x27;s body and instead meticulously taught him the path of immortal cultivation. Their relationship evolved from one of utility to a life-and-death bond of mentor and friend. He risked his power to save Wang Lin on multiple occasions. After Wang Lin helped him reconstruct a physical body, he established the &quot;Nan Wang&quot; (Southern King) faction on Feng Luan Star. He later reincarnated on the Xian Gang Continent as the Grand Marshal &quot;Si Nan&quot; of the Wu Xuan Country, was enfeoffed as the Southern King, finally fulfilling his dream of becoming a prince. 1 Si Tu Nan&#x27;s legend begins with the destiny of his reincarnation as the Green Soul and ends with the freedom of his own choices. This special origin bestowed upon him two major traits: first, a monstrous cultivation aptitude, advancing from the Condensation stage to the Foundation Building stage in just over a month, and creating the legend of &quot;breaking through five realms in three years of secluded cultivation&quot; from Core Formation to Nascent Soul ; second, the shackles of destiny as a vessel for resurrection, burdened from birth with the mission of becoming the reincarnation vessel for the Seven-Colored Immortal Venerable. This inherent contradiction runs through his entire life. 2 As the reincarnation of the Green Soul, Si Tu Nan was initially born as a mortal in a small border country of Vermilion Bird Star. Even in his childhood, he displayed an instinctive resistance to immortal cultivation, wholeheartedly yearning for a life of secular power and wealth. This &quot;anti-cultivation&quot; worldview is laid bare in his classic declaration: &quot;The purpose of my cultivation is to seize all the beautiful women under heaven, plunder all the treasures under heaven, and kill everyone I don&#x27;t like.&quot; However, the gears of fate began to turn from his first encounter with a cultivator — forced onto the path of cultivation to save his own life, yet erupting with astonishing potential during every life-and-death crisis. 2 Character Image True Form Appearance Si Tu Nan possesses a head of red hair and a face with sharp, chiseled features, giving an impression of determination and ruggedness. Three star points slowly rotate at the center of his brow, a unique mark that makes him appear even more mysterious and distinct. Furthermore, his skin is bronze in color, covered with fine, fragmented lines, adding a sense of weathering and highlighting the richness and extraordinariness of his experiences. 2 Avatar Appearance Si Tu Nan&#x27;s avatar appearance differs noticeably from his true form, featuring silver hair and crimson pupils, with a flamboyant hairstyle. The overall look is more cool, dazzling, and eye-catching, showcasing a different kind of domineering and unruliness. 2 Arrogant and Unrestrained Si Tu Nan often uses extreme statements to assert himself, such as claiming his purpose in cultivation is to &quot;dominate all the beauties under heaven, seize treasures, and kill those who displease him.&quot; He shows no fear when facing powerful enemies; he even berated the Fourteenth Generation Vermilion Bird Master with his damaged soul, forcing the latter to sever his own finger and flee merely by the mention of Si Tu Nan&#x27;s name, highlighting his deep-seated, bone-deep arrogance. He bluntly states that cultivation means &quot;defying the heavens and remaining true to one&#x27;s heart,&quot; scorning the ascetic principles of cultivating a pure heart and few desires. His ideals (such as his obsession with glory and wealth, and repeatedly &quot;running away&quot; to avoid battles) are essentially a rebellion against the proposition that &quot;cultivation equals transcendence,&quot; forming the unique spiritual core of his character. Si Tu Nan&#x27;s arrogance is not limited to words but is also manifested in his trampling of rules. He once cursed the Heavenly Dao because &quot;the rain soaked his clothes,&quot; and even deliberately barged into a forbidden area during his Nascent Soul stage just to test if &quot;my fist can break the restrictions.&quot; 2 Deeply Loyal and Righteous Si Tu Nan&#x27;s loyalty is not the traditional kind of &quot;loyalty and righteousness&quot;; it carries the fierce, untamed spirit of the jianghu (martial world). His deep loyalty and righteousness are etched into his bones, manifesting in three extreme forms. The first is the obsession of a promise worth a thousand pieces of gold: to fulfill a promise to his close friend Ye Wuyou, he willingly gave up his grand ambition of traveling the stars, spent a thousand years of effort to elevate the Vermilion Bird Country to a Level Six cultivation country, and even used his own vital essence to seal the Cultivation Star Crystal. When the Third Generation Vermilion Bird Master betrayed him, he would rather have his physical body destroyed than fail to protect Ye Wuyou&#x27;s dying wish. This near &quot;blind loyalty&quot; persistence represents the ultimate value he places on friendship, unwavering even unto death. The second is a doting, fiercely protective attitude, especially towards his disciple Wang Lin : this bond has long surpassed that of an ordinary master and disciple, resembling more an elder brother&#x27;s unconditional protection of a younger sibling. He once exhausted his soul power to help Wang Lin form his Nascent Soul; in the Ancient God&#x27;s Land, even with only a remnant soul, he forced the Heaven Defying Pearl to resist Tuo Sen, roaring to declare his claim: &quot;The disciple I&#x27;ve taken a fancy to, how dare you, a demonic thought, lay a hand on him!&quot; This &quot;domineering protection of his own&quot; remained fervent even after his reincarnation as Si Nan—when Wang Lin was in danger on the Xian Gang Continent, he stepped forward regardless of his unrecovered cultivation. The third is a brotherly loyalty filled with the ethos of the jianghu: whether it was fighting alongside Qing Shui and others against cultivators from outside the realm, willingly damaging the origin of his Green Soul to buy a chance for his brothers; or after being poisoned on Feng Luan Star, laughing heartily and telling Wang Lin: &quot;In this life of mine, what I&#x27;m most proud of is not being a Vermilion Bird Master, but taking on a disciple who dared to defy the heavens for mortals!&quot; This heroic spirit that disregards life and death is precisely the most dazzling footnote to Si Tu Nan&#x27;s charismatic personality. 2 Mundane Obsessions Si Tu Nan&#x27;s path of cultivation, with its shocking and unconventional &quot;vulgarization&quot; declaration, constitutes a sharp deconstruction of the orthodox values of the cultivation world. He bluntly simplifies his cultivation goals to &quot;beauties, treasures, and killing those who displease him.&quot; This seemingly crude desire is, in reality, a powerful dissolution of grand narratives like &quot;immortality&quot; and &quot;ascending to become a transcendent being.&quot; His actions further practice this path: during his Nascent Soul stage, he suddenly abandoned his arduous cultivation to go become a prince in a mortal kingdom, for the sole reason that &quot;I&#x27;ve cultivated enough, it&#x27;s time to enjoy life.&quot; This practice of &quot;hedonism&quot; is far from simple indulgence; it contains profound existentialist overtones of rebellion. As he told Wang Lin within the Heaven Defying Pearl: &quot;Cultivate my ass, first drink with me,&quot; or declared in the Demonic Spirit Land: &quot;I cultivate immortality precisely for this mouthful of wine right now&quot;—this attitude, which places immediate, mundane pleasure above prolonged, bitter cultivation, explicitly negates the traditional notion that &quot;cultivation equals asceticism&quot; and subverts the inherent logic of the cultivation world that &quot;sacrifices the present life for the afterlife.&quot; Accompanying this is the paradoxical nature of his pursuit of power: while he detests the deceit and intrigue of the cultivation world, he obsessively seeks a sense of existence and control within secular royal authority. This contradiction reaches its peak after his reincarnation as Si Nan—he audaciously kidnapped and replaced the Southern King, leaving behind the bold statement: &quot;In this life, I have no more regrets.&quot; On the surface, this act is a pursuit of secular power, but at a deeper level, it resembles an extreme way of using the tangible, controllable experience of secular power to combat the sense of nihilism brought about by the alienation of power in the cultivation world. It is an alternative manifestation of his unique philosophy of existence. 2 Humorous and Unrestrained Si Tu Nan&#x27;s humor is by no means shallow jesting; it is a unique survival wisdom he uses to confront cruel fate. This wisdom is first reflected in his slang-infused cultivation philosophy: he excels at deconstructing obscure, profound cultivation truths into the vivid language of the common streets. For example, he uses &quot;drinking and chasing women&quot; to illuminate the true meaning of &quot;shattering the self,&quot; and &quot;fighting and seizing treasures&quot; to vividly illustrate the essence of cultivation as &quot;defying the heavens.&quot; This rough yet incisive teaching style often helps Wang Lin break through bottlenecks during moments of mental relaxation. At a deeper level is his self-deprecating black humor, which becomes his spiritual armor in desperate situations. After his physical body was destroyed and he was trapped within the Heaven Defying Pearl, he quipped: &quot;Now I don&#x27;t even have the qualifications to chase women&quot;; when poisoned by the &quot;Ecstasy and Death&quot; toxin and nearing death, he could still tease Wang Lin: &quot;Little Lin, if you dare let me die, I won&#x27;t let you off even as a ghost.&quot; Such self-deprecation not only instantly dispels heavy atmospheres but also reveals, through the jest, his unextinguishable, formidable optimism. Most unique is the absurd sense of breaking the fourth wall in his words and actions, which lends the character a layer of postmodern quality. For instance, declaring to Wang Lin within the Heaven Defying Pearl: &quot;A character in this book like me, how could I die in a place like this&quot;—this metanarrative teasing of his own fictional existence transcends mere plot interaction, becoming a kind of sober recognition and playful resistance to the absurdity of fate. 2 Abilities Special Physique Si Tu Nan is the reincarnation of the Green Soul, one of the seven souls of the Seven-Colored Immortal Venerable. He possesses monstrous cultivation talent: he reached the Foundation Establishment stage in just one month; during the Nascent Soul stage, he broke through five consecutive realms in three years of seclusion; he broke through the traditional shackles at the Eight Revolutions cultivator realm. Qing Shui once commented that his talent was comparable to that of the Seven-Colored Immortal Venerable. He has an instinctive comprehension of the Laws of Destruction and the Laws of Life and Death, creating the &quot;Extinction Finger&quot; and &quot;Yellow Spring Finger&quot; techniques early on; later, he integrated the Laws of Samsara to deduce supernatural abilities such as &quot;Yellow Spring Transformation&quot;. As the reincarnation of the Green Soul, he has a bloodline connection with the Heaven Defying Pearl and can activate its heaven-defying fate-altering function. He once assisted Wang Lin in severing his karma lines during Wang Lin&#x27;s battle against the Ancient Dao while stepping into the heavens. After reincarnation, he retained part of his past life memories. Upon awakening on the Xian Gang Continent, he displayed the ability of the Heavenly Eye, capable of seeing through a cultivator&#x27;s disguise. 2 Core Cultivation Techniques Si Tu Nan&#x27;s core cultivation technique is the &quot;Yellow Spring Ascending Aperture Art&quot;. For instance, using the concept of &quot;placing oneself in a deadly situation to be reborn,&quot; he guided Wang Lin to overcome fear during extreme realm cultivation. 2 Character Experience Previous Life and Vermilion Bird Star Period Si Tu Nan is the reincarnation of the Green Soul, one of the three souls and seven spirits of the Seven-Colored Immortal Venerable. He was exceptionally gifted and cultivated at an extremely rapid pace. He was once the Second Generation Vermilion Bird Master of the Vermilion Bird Country. Relying on his formidable personal strength, he elevated the Vermilion Bird Country to a sixth-level cultivation country and shouldered the responsibility of guarding the Vermilion Bird Star, a duty he maintained for a thousand years. He also successfully sealed the Cultivation Star Crystal. However, his great power attracted the envy and schemes of others. The Third Generation Vermilion Bird Master, in alliance with Tan Lang and others, plotted against him, causing him to suffer severe injuries. With no other choice, Si Tu Nan&#x27;s primordial spirit was forced to retreat into the Heaven Defying Pearl to slumber, awaiting an opportunity for change. 2 Meeting and Journey with Wang Lin Later, Wang Lin accidentally obtained the Heaven Defying Pearl, which awakened Si Tu Nan&#x27;s primordial spirit. Si Tu Nan initially intended to seize Wang Lin&#x27;s body. However, because Wang Lin was merely a mortal at the time and could not be possessed, Si Tu Nan had no choice but to teach Wang Lin the path of immortal cultivation, thus becoming Wang Lin&#x27;s guide on his cultivation journey. He taught Wang Lin everything he knew, imparting his cultivation techniques, experiences, and more, helping Wang Lin grow continuously on the path of immortal cultivation. During this process, the bond between Si Tu Nan and Wang Lin gradually deepened, transforming from an initial relationship of utility into a camaraderie of master and friend who shared life and death. He repeatedly rescued Wang Lin from desperate situations, even at the cost of depleting his own power. On one occasion, to save Wang Lin, he exhausted his strength and fell into slumber once again. 2 Reconstructing a Physical Body and Subsequent Experiences When Wang Lin reached the Infant Transformation stage, he successfully helped Si Tu Nan reconstruct a physical body, restoring his cultivation to the Yang Solid Peak. Having regained his freedom, Si Tu Nan always harbored his dream of becoming a prince. Therefore, he went to Feng Luan Star and established the &quot;Southern Prince&quot; faction, living a life of enjoying worldly luxuries for a period. Afterwards, Si Tu Nan also met Wang Lin on Water Spirit Star, where they shared their experiences with each other, and he continued to provide assistance to Wang Lin at critical moments. In the subsequent story, Si Tu Nan followed Wang Lin through many adventures, participated in major events such as the Sealing Realm War, and together with Wang Lin and others, confronted cultivators from outside the realm and faced the truth of the Cave Mansion World. 2 Final Destiny Finally, Si Tu Nan followed Wang Lin in reincarnating to the Xian Gang Continent. On the Xian Gang Continent, he became the Grand Marshal of Wu Xuan Country, known as &quot;Si Nan,&quot; and was later enfeoffed as the Southern Prince, finally realizing his lifelong wish of becoming a prince.
