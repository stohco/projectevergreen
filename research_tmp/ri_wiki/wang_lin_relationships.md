While at the Sea of Devils, Li Muwan spent most of her time refining the Heavenly Li Dan while Wang Lin spent his time on his cultivation. For the better chance of success in refining, Wang Lin went to Nan Dou City to buy a Dan Furnace. Resulting in some of the greedy Elder's to chase him to the cave where Li Muwan and himself are living for his treasure. Liu Mei /Mu Bingmei[]

Gained a passing interest in Wang Lin when her Avatar met him in the Heng Yue Sect, countless years later her main self tried multiple times to steal his domain by proverbially clawing her way into his Dao Heart. During her last attempt however Wang Lin tricks her instead into consuming a Lust Domain, this results in her Charm ability affecting them both. As a result of this she ends up conceiving Wang Ping. Liu Mei's hatred for Wang Lin grows vastly from this incident. In her madness, she turned their son into a resentful spirit which led to her downfall starting by Wang Lin's revenge and finally, her death by the hand of her teacher. Her last, possibly redeeming, act was to tell Wang Lin how Wang Ping could be cured. She was seen to regularly bring up Li Muwan to torture Wang Lin emotionally, often asking if she truly is all his heart has room for. However given the complicated nature of their relationship it's hard to know whether or not this phrase was indicating a sense of jealousy on Liu Mei's part or just pure sadism.

As a result of all of this, Liu Mei's progenitor and true main body, Saintess Mu Bingmei, has complicated feelings towards Wang Lin. She can be thought of both as an extension to Liu Mei and an individual. She took Zhou Ru as a disciple because of Wang Lin and saved him from the All Seer because of these complicated feelings. Eventually she decides that ultimately she is Wang Ping's mother, whatever else, and completes her Dao Heart. She knows Wang Lin and Wang Ping both cannot forget her existence however and was more than willing to die to set them free of that during Wang Lin's revenge against Daoist Water. However Wang Lin himself had more or less reconciled his own feelings towards her by then and chose to save her life instead.

During the final battle of the war between the Sealed and Outer Realms, Wang Lin corrected one of his enemies by noting Bingmei was not his woman, but she is the mother of his son.

Around the end, she experienced Wang Lin's Dream Dao, where she lived an ordinary life as Liu Mei and married Wang Lin.

Her depth with Wang Lin starting from chapter 1166. It began with a beautiful meeting. Wang Lin saw a figure descend from the sky—a girl whose beauty was otherworldly, her skin like fine, flawless jade. With a calm that seemed to still the air around her, she asked him three profound questions: "What is heaven?" Three times she asked, and his answers, sincere and thoughtful, satisfied her deep curiosity. In that moment, a connection was silently woven. 

Their paths continue at the grand auction on Peng Lai. Qianmei disguised, turning her hair into black, they stand side by side, her beauty receiving gaze from others. They seperated, there is a hidden mysterious request from her, if Wang Lin could send her off if that time comes. Wang Lin promised "I will". Later after battle with Wu Qing, Wang Ling found her again. They sat side by side among the powerful cultivators. As the proceedings began, a gentle breeze stirred, and a few strands of her silken hair brushed softly against Wang Lin. He caught the faint, calming fragrance unique to her—a scent like distant orchids and morning dew. In a voice barely above a whisper, she said she would soon have to leave. Without turning to look at her, his gaze fixed ahead, after some moment Wang Lin quietly said, "I will send you off."

When a beautiful painting of lake and rivers appeared on the auction block, Wang Lin placed a bid that silenced the entire hall. No one dared compete. He acquired the scroll, and for the first time since they sat, he turned to face Li Qianmei. In that moment, he truly saw her—not just as a fellow cultivator, but as a woman of devastating beauty, with tranquil eyes that could make any man's heart falter. He handed her the painting, a parting gift. She accepted it, a soft smile touching her lips. Then, to protect his standing before the others, she revealed her identity as the esteemed genius of the Heaven Breaking Sect for all to see before she departed, her blue hair flowing like a banner of quiet protection.

The encounter between Wang Lin and Li Qianmei after that is a poignant and layered moment of unspoken feelings and symbolic fate. 

After the auction, accompanied by the sound of her flute, Li Qianmei intercepts Wang Lin in the star fog, reasoning with she remember that Wang Lin promised to send her off, ostensibly to say goodbye before she departs for a potentially dangerous mission. Their conversation is tinged with a sense of finality, as she hints they may never meet again. In a gesture of care and perhaps something deeper, she attempts to fulfill her promise of giving him a third gift for answering her questions earlier. She offers him a personal and precious protective bracelet, still warm from her wrist. Wang Lin, however, perceives the weight and intimacy of such a gift. With his characteristic guarded nature and reluctance to incur emotional debt, he refuses, stating it is "too expensive" — a clear metaphor for the unspoken attachment it represents.

When Wang Lin turned to go, Li Qianmei then discards the bracelet into the endless star fog, a dramatic act of letting go. She then returns the painting Wang Lin had once given her. Wang Lin, in turn, leaves her a powerful treasure (a celestial pen with a rune) as a final protective gift, balancing their exchange while maintaining his emotional distance. Which she used later in battle inside spatial crack as an exchange so her blood can be sent to Wang Lin's statue. He then departs accompanied by the sound of her flute while then he checked the painting, now altered to show a dried-up riverbed with two fish sustaining each other—a silent, profound message of companionship in desolation and reliance. His brow frowned and a wry smile painting his lips.

The Bracelet as a Symbol of Inevitable Fate: The most striking twist of fate occurs shortly after. As Wang Lin flies through the vast, infinite, and direction-concealing star fog—a place where losing an object means it is gone forever—he unexpectedly comes across the very same jade bracelet Li Qianmei had thrown away. The probability of this happening is astronomically small, akin to finding a specific grain of sand in an ocean. This is not a coincidence within the narrative logic of xianxia; it is a powerful symbol. The bracelet, a token of her care that he refused, has literally found its way back to him. It signifies that their connection cannot be so easily severed by distance or words. It is an object of karma or fate, implying that their destinies are intertwined and will cross again despite his efforts to remain detached. Wang Lin's final act of sighing and taking the bracelet shows his tacit acceptance of this inexplicable bond, carrying a piece of that unresolved connection with him as he moves toward the expedition with Master Ashen Pine.
