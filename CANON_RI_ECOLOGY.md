## PART 0 — Founding Axioms

### Axiom 1 — The Joss Flame Farm (Type A, C5)

The Cave World is **not** a naturally evolved universe. It was created by the
Seven-Colored Daoist ~100,000 years before Wang Lin's birth as an *artificial farm*
to harvest **Joss Flames** — the cultivation energy generated by mortal faith and
cultivation.

> "The Cave World is created as an artificial farm to harvest Joss Flames (cultivation
> energy from mortal faith). The Cave World's law: Third-Step cultivators cannot
> naturally arise. The Seven-Colored Daoist becomes the world-owner."
> — CANON_RI_TIMELINE.md E02

**Ecological consequence (this is the central ecological truth of the cosmology):**
Every mortal habitat in the Cave World exists *because it produces Joss Flames*, not
despite it. Mortals are livestock; their settlements, farms, trade cities, and even
their wars are *harvested*. The Seven-Colored Daoist siphons 30% of all generated
Joss Flame via the seal.

This means: **mortal population density is not random.** It correlates with the
"spiritual fertility" of a region — high-Qi regions support denser mortal populations
who generate more Joss Flame, who are more efficiently harvested.

### Axiom 2 — The Prime Directive (Type A, C5)

> Reality is objective. Cultivation changes a cultivator's *understanding* of the world,
> not the world itself. The world exists independently of the player.

Ecological consequence: a Qi Condensation rat in Zhao Country exists, has stats, has
behavior, has a nest, has predators — *whether or not the player ever perceives it*.
The ecology layer must simulate, not perform.

### Axiom 3 — The Cultivation Causal Chain (Type A → Type C)

This is the **derivation rule** the rest of the document applies. Where canon gives
us a region's Qi density / spirit-vein status, we derive the rest of the ecosystem
through this chain:

```
Qi density (canon)  →  spirit veins (canon or derived)  →  spirit herbs (derived)
                    →  herbivore spirit beasts (derived) →  predator spirit beasts (derived)
                    →  apex spirit beasts (canon if named, derived tier otherwise)
                    →  sects / cultivators harvesting the chain (canon)
                    →  ruins / remnants of past harvesting (canon timeline)
                    →  mortals producing Joss Flame at the base (Type A axiom)
```

Each step's confidence is at most the minimum of the steps it derives from. A canon
beast (Type A) populates a derived biome (Type C); the beast itself remains Type A,
but its *population estimate* is Type C (derived from biome carrying capacity).

### Axiom 4 — The Sealed Hierarchy Caps Ecology (Type A, C5)

The Realm-Sealing Grand Array (spirit: Heaven-Splitting Axe) caps cultivation at
Heaven Blight / quasi-Third-Step anywhere inside the Sealed Realm. This caps the
ecology too: **no beast inside the Sealed Realm can naturally exceed tier 10
(Heaven Blight).** Beasts at tier 11+ (Third Step) are either (a) from the Outer
Realm, (b) sealed ancient entities, or (c) spoilers of the world's farm structure.

The IAC is **outside** the Sealed Realm and has no such cap — that is why 9-Sun-level
beasts (Grand Empyrean) and Wang Lin's Tenth Sun ascension occur there.

### Axiom 5 — Beast Evolution Is Canon-Mechanical (Type A, C5)

Canon attests at least one explicit beast-evolution pathway:

> Brilliant Golden Fruit (Earth Planet) → fed to Mosquito Beast + Thunder Toad →
> triggers their evolution. (N155, N156, L18)

This means beast evolution is **not speculation** — it is a canon mechanic. The
ecology layer must model it. Other derived pathways (dragon blood → beast
empowerment; Earth Fire Dragon soul extraction; Green Devil devouring → host
breakthrough) are also canon-attested and must be modeled.

## PART 1 — Methodology & Classification

Every ecological entry in this document is tagged with one of five classifications
(matches `BridgingPolicy.java` Classification enum):

| Tier | Name | Confidence | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | CANON_CONCRETE | 5 | Directly attested in canon. Immutable in mod. |
| **B** | CANON_IMPLIED | 4 | Canon strongly implies; mod may fill specifics. |
| **C** | REASONABLE_RECONSTRUCTION | 3 | Canon silent; logically necessary; derived from canon facts. |
| **D** | SPECULATION | 1-2 | Canon silent; derived only from established canon, never invented wholesale. |
| **F** | FORBIDDEN | 0 | Canon or user-corrections forbid. Cannot appear in mod. |

**Golden rule (per user):** *Never fill empty space with generic fantasy content. Any
generated content must be derived from geography, cultivation systems, historical
events, ecology, economy, or social consequences already established by canon.*

**Override rule:** If a later-discovered canon fact contradicts a Type C/D entry, the
canon fact wins. The Type C/D entry is corrected or removed.

## PART 2 — Global Ecology Laws

These laws apply across every region. They are the invariants of the RI ecology layer.

### Law G1 — Mortal Population Density Tracks Spiritual Fertility (Type C, conf 3)

**Derivation:** Axiom 1 (Joss Flame Farm) + canon fact that mortals generate Joss
Flame + economic principle that denser Qi supports denser agriculture.

**Result:** Mortal population density in any region correlates (non-linearly) with
that region's spirit-vein density. A region with 120+ Fire Veins (Heavenly Bull
Continent) can support mortal kingdoms numbering in the billions; a region with a
fragile world-law tier (Zhao Country) supports ~100-family villages.

**Why Type C, not B:** Canon never states the population numbers explicitly. But the
relationship is logically forced by Axiom 1.

### Law G2 — Beast Tier Ceiling = Local Cultivation Ceiling (Type A, conf 5)

**Canon basis:** Realm-Sealing Grand Array caps Sealed Realm at Heaven Blight
(tier 10). The IAC is uncapped. The Outer Realm is uncapped.

**Result:** No naturally-occurring beast inside the Sealed Realm can exceed tier 10.
Beasts at tier 11+ inside the Sealed Realm are either sealed ancient entities (Tu Si's
remnant, Wind Demon, etc.) or spoilers. Beasts in the IAC routinely reach Grand
Empyrean (9-Sun tier).

### Law G3 — Spirit Veins Are The Foundation (Type A, conf 5)

**Canon basis:** "120+ Fire Veins on Heavenly Bull Continent" (L65). "Earth Fire main
vein devoured by Wang Lin at Great Soul Sect" (F13). "Cultivation Planet Crystal
inside Planet Suzaku — the seal-mechanism of the planet itself" (L52).

**Result:** Every region's ecology is anchored on its spirit veins. Veins determine:
(a) herb density, (b) beast tier ceiling (within the local law cap), (c) sect
presence, (d) mortal population (via G1).

### Law G4 — Ruin Ecology (Type C, conf 3)

**Derivation:** The timeline lists 39 era-events, almost all of which leave "remaining
traces" (ruins, remnants, lingering seals). Canon is explicit that the player can
find these ruins (e.g., "The Heng Yue Sect exists as the starting sect. The player can
find the stone bead (containing the Heaven-Defying Bead) where Wang Lin found it"
— CANON_RI_TIMELINE.md L462).

**Result:** Ruins are not empty. A ruin's ecology is determined by:
- The original inhabitants (canon sect / beast / civilization)
- The cause of ruin (battle → undead / resentful spirits; age → moss, scavengers;
  seal → sealed entity still inside)
- The local Qi density (high-Qi ruins spawn more spirit beasts; low-Qi ruins spawn
  mundane scavengers)

**Examples (Type C):**
- Teng Family City ruin → resentful-spirit ecology (massacre site, soul flag remnants)
- Suzaku Tomb → seal-spirit ecology (Cultivation Planet Crystal as anchor)
- Immortal Monarch's Cave Mansion → sealed-demon ecology (Wind Demon still inside)
- Wang Family Village ruin → mourning-herb ecology (Wang Lin's return as Soul Formation
  "to bury the dead" implies he left ritual offerings — Type D)

### Law G5 — Beast Evolution Pathways (Type A, conf 5)

Canon-attested evolution mechanics (must be modeled):

| Pathway | Input | Output | Canon Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brilliant Golden Fruit | Mosquito Beast, Thunder Toad | Evolved form | L18, N155, N156 |
| Dragon Blood | Beast (unspecified) | Empowered form | N52, L78 (Falling Land) |
| Earth Fire Dragon Soul | Cultivator (Wang Lin) | Soul-extracted power | L70, N123 |
| Green Devil Devouring | Cultivator (Wang Lin) | Arcane Void breakthrough | N132 |
| Restriction Mountain enlightenment | Cultivator | "Great Enlightened One" title | L49 |
| Soul Refining | Mountain Valley Tribe natives | Soul Refining Tribe (1M+ people) | L43, L44, F26 |

### Law G6 — The Mortal Cycle (Type A, conf 5)

Wang Lin repeatedly descends to mortal life for cultivation breakthroughs:
- Life-Death Domain breakthrough (~Year 60-80): lives as mortal among mortals
- Cloud Sea village doctor (~Year 500-600): lives as mortal doctor
- Raising Wang Ping on Planet Ran Yun (~Year 200-300): raises son as mortal

**Result:** Mortal zones are *cultivation-relevant* — they are not "low-level content."
The mod must spawn mortal villages in mortal-tier regions and treat them as both
Joss Flame source (Axiom 1) and cultivation-trial site (Law G6).

**Cosmological position:** Third-tier cultivation country on Planet Suzaku, inside the

  could not sustain Qi Condensation / Foundation Establishment cultivators). Estimated

- **Derived (Type C, conf 3):** Total spirit veins in Zhao Country: ~8–15, all minor
  (low-tier). None are mid- or high-tier (the world-law tier caps at fragile).

#### 1.3 Flora
- **Canon-attested (Type A):** None explicitly named for Zhao Country.
- **Derived (Type C, conf 3):** Per Law G3, fragile-tier spirit veins produce
  fragile-tier spirit herbs. These are the herbs that Heng Yue Sect's outer disciples

  - *Qi-Gathering Grass* (Type C) — sustains Qi Condensation cultivation

  Bead (inside a dead bird) likely has unique herb ecology — the bead was hidden in a
  *dead bird*, which implies the cliff supports a small bird population, which implies
  insects, which implies mosses. The bead's presence does NOT make the cliff
  high-Qi (the bead is an external artifact, not a spirit vein).

#### 1.4 Fauna

  - *Stone-Backed Boar* (Type C) — Qi Condensation tier herbivore; roots for

  - *Teng-Clan War Hound* (Type C) — bred by the Teng Clan for guarding; not a spirit
    beast but a trained mortal hound

  selection system implies the Clan has access to Core Formation resources (pills,

  import-route itself is canon-silent.

## PART 6 — Implementation Notes for the Forge Mod

### 6.1 Ecology Layer Architecture (Type C, conf 3)

The ecology layer should be implemented as a `RealityProfile` per region, with fields:
- `worldLawTier` (Type A, from L01-L80)
- `sealedStatus` (Type A, from L05)
- `spiritVeinCount` (Type A if canon-attested, Type C if derived)
- `spiritVeinAspects` (Type A if canon-attested, Type C if derived)
- `floraCatalog` (Type A if canon-named, Type C if derived)
- `faunaCatalog` (Type A if canon-named, Type C if derived)
- `mortalPopulationEstimate` (Type C, derived from Law G1)
- `sectPresence` (Type A, from F01-F45)
- `ruinEcology` (Type C, derived from Law G4)
- `jossFlameProductionRate` (Type C, derived from mortal population)

### 6.2 Beast Spawning Rules (Type A + Type C)

1. Beast tier ceiling = local world-law tier cap (Law G2). Inside Sealed Realm: tier
   10 max. IAC: uncapped.
2. Beast aspect = local spirit-vein aspect (Law G3).
3. Beast population density ∝ spirit-vein density (Type C, conf 3).
4. Beast evolution: implement the Brilliant Golden Fruit pathway (Type A). Other
   pathways (dragon blood, soul extraction, Green Devil devouring) are also canon.
5. Apex beasts: canon-named beasts (Mosquito Beast, Earth Fire Dragon, Green Devil
   Scorpion, etc.) are unique spawns with fixed stats. Derived beasts fill the rest.

### 6.3 Mortal Spawning Rules (Type A + Type C)

1. Mortal population density ∝ spiritual fertility (Law G1).
2. Mortal villages spawn in mortal-tier regions (Type A — Wang Family Village, etc.).
3. Mortal trial villages spawn in mortal-tier regions for cultivation breakthroughs
   (Law G6 / Thread T7).
4. Every mortal generates Joss Flame. Soul Formation+ cultivators can perceive it
   (Type A — JossFlameEconomy.java canPerceiveJossFlames()).
5. The 30% siphon to the world-owner is automatic (Type A).

### 6.4 Ruin Spawning Rules (Type C, conf 3)

1. Ruins spawn at canon-attested ruin locations (Type A — Wang Family Village ruin,
   Teng Family City ruin, Suzaku Tomb, Immortal Monarch's Cave Mansion, Ancient
   Battlefield, Ancient Tomb, etc.).
2. Ruin ecology = f(original inhabitants, cause of ruin, local Qi density) (Law G4).
3. Ruin tier decays over time per Thread T6.
4. Ruins with sealed entities (Wind Demon, Green Devil Scorpion, Dong Lin Pool's
   Ancient God, etc.) persist until the seal breaks (Type A).
