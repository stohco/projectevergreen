   501→     own pace and talk as much as they want without the NPC
   502→     wandering off. "...Yes?" Radial verb-prompts appear. BUT
   503→     THE WORLD KEEPS RUNNING. Birds fly. OTHER NPCs walk.
   504→     Children run by. Li Muwan walks past carrying herbs. The
   505→     sun moves. Rain falls. Only the NPC you are talking to
   506→     holds still for you; everything else is alive. The player
   507→     remains mobile (may walk-and-talk, see § XXXII.6). When the
   508→     player selects "Nevermind" / dismisses the conversation,
   509→     the NPC resumes their schedule and pathing from where they
   510→     paused. This is the default.
   511→
   512→  2. FOCUS — When the conversation actually matters (discussing
   513→     Dao, requesting a Heaven Defying Bead copy, entering a sect,
   514→     negotiating). The camera subtly zooms. Player movement
   515→     disables. The NPC holds still (as in Ambient). BUT THE
   516→     WORLD KEEPS RUNNING. Other NPCs still move. The sun still
   517→     sets. Rain still falls. Rumors continue spreading. Only
   518→     the player stops moving because the player is busy talking.
   519→     Exactly like real life.
   520→
   521→  3. MAJOR CANON MOMENTS — Very rare. Inheritance, tribulation,
   522→     True Body meeting, major revelations. Cinematic framing —
   523→     camera, music, animations, special dialogue. These must be
   524→     incredibly uncommon. Otherwise they lose impact.
   525→
   526→§ XXXII.1a — "Holds Still" Is A Courtesy, Not A Freeze
   527→When an NPC holds still for the player, it is a behavioral
   528→courtesy, not a simulation freeze. The NPC is still perceiving
   529→the world: if a threat appears, they react (§ XXXII.5). If
   530→their schedule has a hard time-bound event (a sect lecture
   531→they must attend, a tribulation window), they may break the
   532→courtesy: "I have to go." and resume pathing. The courtesy
   533→expires on (a) player dismiss ("Nevermind"), (b) NPC
   534→hard-deadline, or (c) world interruption (combat, disaster).
   535→It does NOT expire on idle — the NPC will wait patiently
   536→because that is what a person does when someone is talking
   537→to them.
   538→
   539→§ XXXII.2 — Conversation Affordances, Not Dialogue Trees
   540→The interface shows VERBS, not dialogue options. Not "Tell me
   541→about yourself. Where are you from? Goodbye." Instead:
   542→Discuss, Request, Teach Me, Offer, Trade, Travel Together,
   543→Observe Technique, Challenge, Give Gift, Ask Opinion, Ask About...
   544→
   545→Each verb expands per-NPC. The same verb produces entirely
   546→different sub-menus for different NPCs:
   547→  Wang Lin → Request → {Technique, Artifact Copy, Advice,
   548→                         Restriction, Memory, Training}
   549→  Li Muwan → Request → {Pill, Herb Identification, Medical
   550→                         Advice, Alchemy Lesson}
   551→  Xu Liguo → Request → {Scout, Possess, Steal, Distract,
   552→                         Cause Trouble}
   553→Same system. Entirely different UI. No special-casing — the
   554→affordances are data the UI discovers (Article XXVIII).
   555→
   556→§ XXXII.3 — Requests Are Simulations, Not Transactions
   557→Asking Wang Lin for the God Slaying Sword does not instantly
   558→receive it. Wang Lin THINKS. He evaluates relationship, current
   559→cultivation, knowledge, judgment, intent, history. Then:
   560→  "..." / "No." / "You wouldn't survive using it."
   561→  OR "I can. But not yet. Learn this first." (hands something else)
   562→  OR "...Very well." (True Body manufactures a copy, clone
   563→      retrieves it, hands it over)
   564→  OR "If you still want it after Mosquito Valley..." (a challenge
   565→      begins naturally, no quest log)
   566→
   567→§ XXXII.4 — Fuzzy Asking
   568→The player does not select from item IDs. The player types or
   569→selects intent: "I want something that helps fight souls." The
   570→NPC REASONS about the intent — not a database search — and
   571→chooses: maybe a Soul Banner, maybe a Soul Restriction, maybe
   572→"Not yet." The NPC's response reflects their judgment of what
   573→the player needs, not what the player asked for verbatim.
   574→
   575→§ XXXII.5 — Conversations Are Interruptible
   576→If something happens — thunder, sect attack, spirit beast,
   577→tribulation, explosion, enemy arrives — NPCs react. Wang Lin
   578→stops talking mid-sentence. "Later." Draws his weapon. The
   579→conversation ends naturally because the world kept moving.
   580→The player never entered a frozen dialogue world.
   581→
   582→§ XXXII.6 — Walk And Talk
   583→NPCs do not stop to converse. Wang Lin says "Come." and walks
   584→toward a mountain. The player walks beside him. The conversation
   585→UI stays open in the corner. Both move through the world. He
   586→points out a restriction on a cliff. A spirit beast appears.
   587→The conversation naturally pauses while dealing with it. Then
   588→resumes. This is how conversations happen in Renegade Immortal
   589→— not standing face-to-face in a frozen box.
   590→
   591→§ XXXII.7 — Personality Changes The Interface
   592→The same conversation system renders differently per-NPC:
   593→  Wang Lin — very few words, lots of silence, observes you,
   594→              long pauses before responding.
   595→  Xu Liguo — interrupts constantly, makes jokes, changes
   596→              topics, lies.
   597→  Li Muwan — detailed, patient, explains thoroughly.
   598→  Situ Nan — laughs, mocks, walks away mid-conversation.
   599→  Qing Shui — may answer "...". For thirty seconds.
   600→This is personality through interaction, not just dialogue text.
   601→
   602→§ XXXII.8 — Conversations Are Stateful
   603→If the player asked Wang Lin about restrictions yesterday, he
   604→remembers. If he promised to teach after Foundation Establishment,
   605→that option is unavailable until then. If he refused the God
   606→Slaying Sword request because the player wasn't ready, he won't
   607→pretend it's the first time. Conversations evolve as relationship,
   608→reputation, knowledge, and cultivation change.
   609→
   610→§ XXXII.9 — Conversations Are World Events
   611→Dialogue is not outside the world. A conversation is an event
   612→with a location, volume, and witnesses. Nearby NPCs overhear.
   613→Beliefs update. Rumors may form. Relationships change. History
   614→records it. Loudly asking Wang Lin about the Heaven Defying Bead
   615→in the middle of Heng Yue Sect is potentially dangerous — someone
   616→may overhear fragments, grow suspicious, follow the player. A
   617→private discussion in a secluded cave has different consequences.
   618→The location of a conversation is a gameplay variable.
   619→
   620→Article XXXIII — Motivations, Not Desires
   621→Article XXXI.1 named the per-cycle NPC state "desire." That word
   622→is too narrow. Many interactions are motivated by things that are
   623→not desires at all: fears, duties, debts, grudges, survival.
   624→
   625→The per-cycle NPC state is hereby renamed MOTIVATION STATE.
   626→The ten social engines remain (Art XXXI.1) — they are the
   627→expression layer. What feeds them is the motivation state,
   628→which draws from a richer vocabulary of DRIVING FORCES:
   629→
   630→  ambitions, fears, duties, debts, promises, grudges,
   631→  curiosity, love, loyalty, survival, greed, vengeance,
   632→  sect orders, cultivation bottlenecks, opportunities.
   633→
   634→§ XXXIII.1 — Every Interaction Must Have A Reason
   635→Nobody wakes up wanting to talk to the player. Everyone wakes
   636→with problems, ambitions, fears, obligations, loyalties, and
   637→opportunities. Then they ask:
   638→  "Who, if anyone, can help me?"
   639→  "Who is standing in my way?"
   640→The player MIGHT be the answer. The player might not. The player
   641→is relevant only when circumstances make them relevant.
   642→
   643→§ XXXIII.2 — The Player Is Not Default Relevant
   644→Wang Lin's early priorities: protect family, become stronger,
   645→avoid attracting attention, acquire resources, study restrictions.
   646→Notice what is missing: the player. Until the player becomes
   647→relevant — by demonstrating a skill, owing a debt, holding a
   648→grudge, or standing in the way — Wang Lin has no motivation
   649→directed at the player. This is not coldness. This is how Er Gen
   650→characters actually behave. Relevance is earned, not assumed.
   651→
   652→§ XXXIII.3 — Relevance Is Earned Through Circumstance
   653→The player becomes relevant when an NPC's driving force intersects
   654→with the player's demonstrated behavior:
   655→  - Player excellent with restrictions + Wang Lin needs help
   656→    deciphering a restriction → player becomes relevant.
   657→  - Player knows herbs + Li Muwan needs Crimson Root → player
   658→    becomes relevant.
   659→  - Player helped Wang Lin + Wang Lin owes a favor → player
   660→    becomes relevant (Wang Lin may seek the player out).
   661→  - Player stole from merchant + merchant has a grievance →
   662→    player becomes relevant (merchant may warn others, refuse
   663→    trade, or set a trap).
   664→Relevance is never granted. It is earned or incurred.
   665→
   666→Article XXXIV — Relationships Are Graphs
   667→A relationship is not a number. A relationship is a graph of
   668→distinct, independently-variable dimensions. "Friendship > 50"
   669→is forbidden. NPCs maintain structured relationship state.
   670→
   671→§ XXXIV.1 — The Relationship Graph
   672→Every NPC maintains a per-target relationship graph with at
   673→minimum:
   674→  Trust (0-100)        — does the NPC believe the target's words
   675→  Respect (0-100)      — does the NPC esteem the target's capability
   676→  Fear (0-100)         — does the NPC fear the target
   677→  Debt (signed int)    — outstanding favors owed (+ = NPC owes
   678→                         target, - = target owes NPC)
   679→  Grievance (0-100)    — accumulated resentment from wrongs
   680→  Shared History (int) — count of significant shared events
   681→  Known Skills [list]  — skills the NPC has observed the target use
   682→  Known Personality [list] — traits the NPC has inferred
   683→                         (e.g. "keeps promises", "doesn't betray
   684→                         allies", "greedy", "merciful")
   685→
   686→Two NPCs can have completely different graphs for the same
   687→player. The village elder's graph (Trust 82, Respect 71, Fear
   688→10, Grievance 0) is a different relationship than Teng
   689→Huayuan's graph (Trust 5, Fear 88, Grievance 64, Respect 91).
   690→The simulation treats them as different relationships because
   691→they ARE different relationships.
   692→
   693→§ XXXIV.2 — Favors Are Callable Debts
   694→A favor is not "relationship += 20." A favor is a structured
   695→debt that persists and can be called in later:
   696→  Owner: who did the favor
   697→  Recipient: who received the benefit
   698→  Magnitude: Minor | Moderate | Major | Life-Saving
   699→  Reason: what happened
   700→  Expires: when (Never for Life-Saving)
   701→  Callable: can the owner demand repayment
   702→  Called: has it been called (and was it honored)
   703→
   704→Wang Lin once saved the player from a Core Formation cultivator.
   705→That is a Major favor, never expires. Years later, the player
   706→asks to study the Ji Realm. Wang Lin reasons:
   707→  "Player once risked his life for me. Player has shown
   708→   restraint. Player has sufficient cultivation. Teaching
   709→   benefits him. I owe him. Yes."
   710→The favor is called. The debt is honored. This feels earned
   711→because it IS earned — the simulation tracked the favor for
   712→years and surfaced it at the right moment.
   713→
   714→§ XXXIV.3 — Grievances Persist And Spread
   715→A grievance is not forgotten when the player leaves the area.
   716→If the player steals a spirit stone, the merchant remembers.
   717→The village remembers. A rumor spreads. People become cautious.
   718→Li Muwan may still trust the player (her graph is independent),
   719→but the merchant does not. Grievances feed the RumorEngine
   720→(Art XXXI.1) and shape future interactions. A grievance may
   721→decay if amends are made, but it never silently resets.
   722→
   723→§ XXXIV.4 — The Deconfliction Principle
   724→When multiple NPCs have motivations that could target the
   725→player simultaneously, they do NOT all approach. Each reasons
   726→independently:
   727→  NPC A: "Player is busy. Tomorrow."
   728→  NPC B: "The merchant can help instead. I'll ask him."
   729→  NPC C: "I'll gather the herbs myself."
   730→  NPC D: "I'll ask Wang Lin."
   731→  NPC E: "Not urgent."
   732→  NPC F: "Player owes me. I'll go now."
   733→Only NPC F actually approaches. The others resolved their
   734→motivation through alternative means. This is the "everyone
   735→lining up" problem, solved by the same reasoning engine that
   736→governs every other NPC decision. The player is never mobbed.

   739→THE CHARACTER-FIRST & UNIVERSAL INTERACTION AMENDMENTS
   740→(Articles XXXV–XXXVIII)
   741→Added when the project recognized that even motivation-driven
   742→simulation is still simulation-first. The world should not run
   743→on motivations. It should run on people. Wang Lin should not
   744→wake up and ask "which motivation has the highest score?" He
   745→should ask "What do I believe is happening? What kind of person
   746→am I? What matters today? What options do I actually have?"
   747→And the common language through which every entity — player,
   748→NPC, beast, artifact, sect, location — engages the world is a
   749→single universal object: the Interaction.
   750→═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
   751→
   752→Article XXXV — Character-First, Not Simulation-First
   753→The world does not run on motivations. The world runs on people.
   754→Motivation is one ingredient in a character's reasoning, not
   755→the driver of it. The old pipeline (Motivation → Interaction)
   756→is superseded.
   757→
   758→The character reasoning pipeline is:
   759→  Perception → Beliefs → Relationships → Identity →
   760→  Current Circumstances → Opportunities → Prediction →
   761→  Intent → Plan → Action
   762→
   763→§ XXXV.1 — What A Character Asks Each Cycle
   764→Every character, each reasoning cycle, asks:
   765→  - What do I believe is happening? (Perception → Beliefs)
   766→  - What kind of person am I? (Identity)
   767→  - What matters today? (Identity + Circumstances)
   768→  - What options do I actually have? (Opportunities)
   769→  - What do I predict will happen if I act? (Prediction)
   770→  - What will I attempt? (Intent → Plan → Action)
   771→
   772→Motivation is one input to Intent — alongside beliefs,
   773→relationships, identity, circumstances, and prediction.
   774→It does not disappear, but it is no longer the apex.
   775→
   776→§ XXXV.2 — Relevance Is Universal, Not Player-Special
   777→The old `player_relevance` field (Article XXXIII) special-cased
   778→the player. That is forbidden. Relevance is calculated for EVERY
   779→actor a character can perceive, using the same system:
   780→  relevance(target) = f(perceived_threat, perceived_opportunity,
   781→                       shared_history, current_goals_alignment,
   782→                       power_differential, recent_interactions)
   783→
   784→Wang Lin calculates relevance for Li Muwan, Situ Nan, Teng
   785→Huayuan, a random disciple, and the player using the same
   786→function. The player is a variable, not a special case. This
   787→makes the system extensible to the entire Er Gen multiverse
   788→without per-player code paths.
   789→
   790→§ XXXV.3 — Understanding Is A Relationship Dimension
   791→The relationship graph (Article XXXIV) is extended with a new
   792→dimension: Understanding (0-100). This is NOT Respect — you can
   793→respect someone you do not understand, and understand someone
   794→you do not respect.
   795→  - Wang Lin trusts Situ Nan but does not always understand him.
   796→  - Li Muwan understands Wang Lin extremely well.
   797→  - Xu Liguo misunderstands everyone constantly.
   798→Understanding shapes dialogue: low understanding means the NPC
   799→misinterprets the player's intent, responds to what they THOUGHT
   800→was said, and may agree to the wrong thing or refuse the right
   801→one. High understanding means the NPC reads subtext and responds
   802→to what the player meant, not just what they said.
   803→
   804→Article XXXVI — The Universal Interaction
   805→There is one universal object through which every entity in the
   806→game engages every other entity: the INTERACTION. Every NPC,
   807→item, beast, plant, technique, formation, location, artifact,
   808→and sect exposes a set of interactions. The player, NPCs, beasts,
   809→and even the world itself initiate interactions. The affordance
   810→system (Article XXVIII) and the social engines (Article XXXI.1)
   811→are now unified under this single object.
   812→
   813→§ XXXVI.1 — What Is An Interaction
   814→An interaction is a bidirectional engagement between two entities
   815→(initiator and target) with: a verb, an intent, a context, a
   816→negotiation, and an outcome. It is not a quest, not a dialogue
   817→option, not a transaction. It is the atomic unit of all
   818→engagement in the world.
   819→
   820→§ XXXVI.2 — The Interaction Catalog
   821→Every entity exposes interactions from a shared catalog. The
   822→catalog includes but is not limited to:
   823→  Ask, Teach, Trade, Threaten, Observe, Challenge, Gift, Borrow,
   824→  Copy, Cultivate Together, Explore Together, Travel Together,
   825→  Spar, Escort, Protect, Hire, Recruit, Investigate, Confess,
   826→  Lie, Deceive, Negotiate, Call Debt, Repay Debt, Warn, Reveal
   827→  Secret, Share Memory, Study Together, Meditate Together,
   828→  Request, Offer, Demonstrate, Follow, Block, Approach, Leave.
   829→
   830→Two entities are mechanically unique when they expose different
   831→interaction sets (Article XXVIII, extended). The UI discovers
   832→these. The AI uses these as its language for engaging the world.
   833→
   834→§ XXXVI.3 — Conversations Are Negotiations
   835→When the player initiates an interaction (e.g., "Teach me
   836→restrictions"), the NPC does not simply grant or refuse. The NPC
   837→reasons as a planner: pros, cons, alternatives, risk. Then the
   838→NPC responds as a negotiator:
   839→  - "Not yet. Learn formations first." (redirect, not refusal)
   840→  - "I can. But not until you reach Foundation Establishment." (conditional)
   841→  - "...Very well. But only the first layer." (partial grant)
   842→  - "No. You would die." (refusal with reason)
   843→  - "If you still want it after Mosquito Valley..." (defer + challenge)
   844→The NPC is trying to satisfy their own goals while engaging the
   845→player's request. This is how Wang Lin actually converses.
   846→
   847→§ XXXVI.4 — Interactions Are The AI's Language
   848→NPCs do not have a separate system for "what to do with the
   849→player." They use interactions to engage everything: Wang Lin
   850→asks Li Muwan for herbs (interaction: Request), a sect elder
   851→challenges a disciple (interaction: Challenge), a beast stalks
   852→a spirit rabbit (interaction: Hunt — a beast-specific interaction).
   853→The same object, the same reasoning, every direction. This is
   854→what makes the world feel inhabited rather than simulated.
   855→
   856→Article XXXVII — The Conversation UI (Three Layers + Learning)
   857→The conversation UI must solve the hardest design problem in the
   858→project: the player does not know what they don't know. Showing
   859→every possible interaction spoils discovery. Making everything
   860→free-form typing leaves 99% of players lost. The answer is
   861→neither — it is three layers, with a UI that learns.
   862→
   863→§ XXXVII.1 — Layer 1: Obvious Actions
   864→When the player right-clicks an NPC, a small clean verb wheel
   865→appears: Observe, Talk, Ask, Offer, Request, Train, Travel,
   866→Leave. That is all. No spoilers. "Teach me Ji Realm" and "Copy
   867→God Slaying Sword" do NOT appear, because the character does not
   868→yet know those are possibilities. The wheel is small enough to
   869→read in a glance and clean enough to feel like talking to a
   870→person, not browsing a database.
   871→
   872→§ XXXVII.2 — Layer 2: Context Expansion
   873→Clicking a Layer 1 verb drills down. "Ask" expands to: About
   874→this place / About cultivation / About recent events / About
   875→himself / Something else... The submenu is PER-NPC — asking
   876→Wang Lin about cultivation yields (Beginner advice / Current
   877→bottleneck / Recommended techniques / Breakthroughs) while
   878→asking Li Muwan yields (Herbs / Pills / Poison / Healing).
   879→Same UI, different affordances, discovered from data.
   880→
   881→§ XXXVII.3 — Layer 3: Natural Language
   882→Every submenu ends with "Something else..." which opens a free
   883→text input: "What do you want to ask?" The player may type
   884→"Can you teach me restrictions?" or "Can I borrow your mosquito
   885→swarm?" or "Would you come with me?" The NPC's reasoning
   886→interprets the intent. If the NPC cannot understand, they say
   887→"I don't understand." — exactly like talking to a real person.
   888→~95% of interactions happen through Layers 1-2; ~5% through
   889→Layer 3. Typing feels powerful, not mandatory.
   890→
   891→§ XXXVII.4 — The Menu Learns
   892→The UI evolves with the character's knowledge and relationship.
   893→Before observing Wang Lin study restrictions: "Ask → Cultivation
   894→/ Recent Events / Himself." After weeks of observation: "Ask →
   895→Cultivation / Restriction Formations / Recent Events / Himself."
   896→A new branch appeared because the character learned. Before
   897→becoming friends: "Request → Directions / Advice / Spar / Leave."
   898→After becoming sworn brothers: "Request → Teach Technique / Copy
   899→Capability / Help Breakthrough / Explore Together / Hold Item /
   900→Leave." The UI is a reflection of the character's place in the
   901→world, not a static list.
   902→
   903→§ XXXVII.5 — The UI Reflects Character Knowledge, Not World State
   904→The player cannot request "God Slaying Sword" until they have
   905→witnessed it. First they see "a strange sword" (Request → Ask
   906→about the strange sword). Later, after learning its name:
   907→"Request → Ask about the God Slaying Sword." Eventually, after
   908→becoming close: "Request → Request a copy of the God Slaying
   909→Sword." The UI tracks what the CHARACTER knows, not what the
   910→world contains. This preserves discovery without hiding the
   911→mechanic.
   912→
   913→§ XXXVII.6 — NPC Reasoning Gates UI Options
   914→An interaction option appears only if the NPC's current
   915→reasoning judges it possible. "Teach me Ji Realm" does NOT
   916→appear while Wang Lin has decided the player would die. When
   917→the player reaches Core Formation, Wang Lin re-evaluates, and
   918→the option appears. Nothing changed in the UI — the world
   919→changed. This is the protagonist's growth made visible through
   920→the interface.
   921→
   922→§ XXXVII.7 — Read The Room
   923→Holding right-click for half a second (before the verb wheel
   924→opens) shows a perception overlay: Current mood, Appears to be
   925→[doing], Seems busy, Seems willing to talk, Current concern,
   926→Relationship (in natural language: "Mutual respect", "Trusted
   927→friend", "Wary stranger"). No numbers. No gamey stats. Just
   928→what the character can infer. This tells the player whether
   929→this is a good time to ask for something — and whether the
   930→NPC is even receptive. It is the missing social-perception
   931→layer that makes the world feel inhabited.
   932→
   933→Article XXXVIII — Capability Compatibility (The Protagonist Cheat)
   934→The protagonist's cheat is not "copy everything." It is
   935→Capability Compatibility: the ability to perfectly integrate
   936→a copy of a technique or artifact IF (a) the source is willing
   937→to transmit, AND (b) the protagonist's cultivation can withstand
   938→it, AND (c) the protagonist's soul can integrate it.
   939→
   940→§ XXXVIII.1 — The Three Gates
   941→Every capability-copy attempt passes three gates:
   942→  1. WILLINGNESS — the source NPC must be willing (judged by
   943→     their reasoning: relationship, understanding, motive,
   944→     favor ledger, judgment of the requester's readiness).
   945→  2. WITHSTAND — the protagonist's cultivation realm must be
   946→     sufficient to survive the technique's backlash. The God
   947→     Slaying Sword at Qi Condensation = soul destruction.
   948→     At Nascent Soul = survivable.
   949→  3. INTEGRATE — the protagonist's soul must be able to hold
   950→     the capability. This is cultivation-tier-gated per
   951→     capability. A First Step soul cannot hold a Third Step
   952→     technique, even if willing and even if the body survives.
   953→
   954→§ XXXVIII.2 — Why This Is Elegant
   955→The cheat is powerful but bounded by the world's reality. The
   956→protagonist cannot shortcut to godhood — they must cultivate
   957→to the tier where each capability becomes compatible. This
   958→matches canon (Wang Lin grows into his copied abilities over
   959→thousands of chapters, not instantly). It also makes the
   960→cheat feel earned rather than granted: the player watches the
   961→UI option appear only when all three gates pass, and knows
   962→the world judged them ready.

   965→THE MOMENTUM AMENDMENT
   966→(Article XXXIX)
   967→Added when the project recognized that an authored world with
   968→history is still a diorama. History records what happened.
   969→Momentum records what is STILL happening — right now, before the
   970→player arrives, and will continue happening whether or not the
   971→player ever intervenes. The leap is from "authored world" to
   972→"authored world with inertia." Once every system has momentum
   973→before the player ever joins, the world stops feeling like a game
   974→level and starts feeling like a place that genuinely exists
   975→independently of the player.
   976→═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
   977→
   978→Article XXXIX — Reality Has Momentum
   979→The world is not merely authored, and it is not merely historical.
   980→Every system must possess ongoing state and direction independent
   981→of the player's presence. Rivers continue to erode, spirit veins
   982→continue to fluctuate, caravans continue to travel, sects continue
   983→to recruit, grudges continue to fester, herbs continue to ripen,
   984→and opportunities continue to emerge. The player enters a world
   985→already in motion and can alter its trajectory, but never serve as
   986→the origin of motion itself.
   987→
   988→§ XXXIX.1 — History Versus Momentum
   989→History is a record: "The bridge over Zhao River collapsed in
   990→the winter of Wang Lin's fourteenth year." Momentum is a live
   991→trajectory: "The village is slowly dying because trade never
   992→recovered after the bridge collapse — three families have already
   993→left, the merchant visits half as often, and the elder is
   994→quietly considering whether to ask Heng Yue Sect for aid." Both
   995→are true. History is what happened. Momentum is what is still
   996→happening AS A CONSEQUENCE, right now, in the present tick. A
   997→living world has both. A dead world has only history.
   998→
   999→§ XXXIX.2 — Every System Has A Trajectory
  1000→Every simulated system — not just NPCs — must declare its
  1001→momentum: a current state, a rate of change, a set of drivers,
  1002→a set of thresholds, and a set of consequences when a threshold
  1003→is crossed. This applies to:
  1004→  - Spirit veins (qi density rising or falling, fluctuation
  1005→    period, depletion risk, recovery rate)
  1006→  - Sects (recruitment rate, resource drain, faction tension,
  1007→    ideological drift, decline or ascent)
  1008→  - Caravans and patrols (position along route, supplies
  1009→    remaining, threat exposure, ETA, morale)
  1010→  - Cultivators approaching breakthrough (progress, backlash
  1011→    risk, window of vulnerability, accumulation rate)
  1012→  - Herbs and spirit flora (ripeness, potency, maturation ETA,
  1013→    decay if not harvested, mutation under spirit density)
  1014→  - Bandit and beast groups (food supply, desperation, raid
  1015→    cadence, territory size, prey density)
  1016→  - Rumors and reputation (spread vector, belief intensity,
  1017→    decay, distortion per retelling, reach)
  1018→  - Rivers and terrain (erosion rate, flood risk, course
  1019→    drift, spirit-mineral deposition)
  1020→  - Factions and grudges (festering rate, flashpoint proximity,
  1021→    catalysts, decay under amends)
  1022→  - Economies (price drift, supply chain stress, scarcity
  1023→    emergence, speculation bubbles)
  1024→A system WITHOUT a declared momentum is forbidden from being
  1025→called "living." It is scenery.
  1026→
  1027→§ XXXIX.3 — The Player Intersects Trajectories, Never Originates Them
  1028→When the player arrives at Wang Family Village, the world is
  1029→already mid-sentence. Old Chen's dog is already old. The wolf
  1030→pack has already been pressing closer for two seasons. The
  1031→village's spirit vein has already been thinning for a decade.
  1032→The Heng Yue recruiter is already en route and three days out.
  1033→Wang Lin has already been studying the cave restriction for
  1034→weeks and is already stuck on the third layer. The player did
  1035→not cause any of this. The player arrives into it. The player's
  1036→choices can accelerate, divert, or resolve these trajectories —
  1037→but the trajectories were moving before the player existed and,
  1038→under Article V, would continue moving if the player never
  1039→existed.
  1040→
  1041→§ XXXIX.4 — Momentum Is Compiled, Not Scripted
  1042→Momentum is not a scripted event sequence ("on day 7, the wolf
  1043→attack happens"). Momentum is a compiled property of the world
  1044→state, derived from the World Blueprint and advanced every
  1045→simulation tick by the drivers declared in each system's
  1046→momentum schema. The same blueprint, given a different
  1047→Simulation Seed and a different player arrival time, produces a
  1048→different present moment — not because the geography changed
  1049→(it never does) but because the trajectories have advanced to
  1050→different points. This is the source of replayability under the
  1051→authored-world model: the stage is fixed, the actors are mid-
  1052→motion, and WHERE in the motion the player arrives varies.
  1053→
  1054→§ XXXIX.5 — The Momentum Test (Living Chapter Bar)
  1055→A chapter is not living unless, at the moment the player first
  1056→arrives, at least THREE systems are visibly mid-trajectory and
  1057→the player can perceive at least one of them WITHOUT being told.
  1058→Examples of perceivable momentum without notification:
  1059→  - The player sees a caravan's wagons in the distance, already
  1060→    moving — nobody assigned a quest.
  1061→  - The player overhears two villagers discussing a rumor that
  1062→    has clearly been circulating for days (it has details, age,
  1063→    and disagreement).
  1064→  - The player notices the shrine incense is fresh though the
  1065→    shrine is abandoned — someone was here recently and will
  1066→    return.
  1067→  - The player finds a half-harvested herb garden — the
  1068→    cultivator is mid-task and will return.
  1069→If everything the player perceives was placed there FOR the
  1070→player to perceive, the world has no momentum. It has dressing.
  1071→
  1072→§ XXXIX.6 — Momentum Must Be Interruptible And Redirectable
  1073→The player (and NPCs, and world events) can alter a trajectory.
  1074→A weakening spirit vein can be restored by a formation. A
  1075→festering grudge can be settled by a mediation. A ripening herb
  1076→can be harvested early (losing potency) or guarded (gaining
  1077→potency but risking discovery). A caravan can be raided (its
  1078→trajectory ends; the village it was supplying begins a scarcity
  1079→trajectory). Momentum is not a cutscene. It is a live vector
  1080→the world holds, and any sufficiently strong intervention
  1081→redirects it. The redirect itself becomes history and seeds new
  1082→momentum.
  1083→
  1084→§ XXXIX.7 — The Architectural Consequence
  1085→This article requires the architectural inversion the project
  1086→has been approaching: the world is authored as a COMPILED STATE
  1087→with momentum, not as a set of objects placed at chunk-load
  1088→time. Chunks render a world that already exists in the compiled
  1089→state. The simulation queries a world database (spatial index,
  1090→relationship graph, travel graph, spirit graph, hydrology
  1091→graph, settlement graph, influence map) — not raw JSON. The
  1092→compiler turns the World Blueprint DSL into this compiled state,
  1093→seeds every system's momentum to its canonical initial
  1094→trajectory, and the simulation advances it every tick. Terrain
  1095→is the LAST concern, because terrain is merely the block-level
  1096→visualization of a world whose semantic state (traversable,
  1097→steep, river, forest, spirit-rich) already exists in the
  1098→compiled state. Build the living state first. Render it last.
  1099→

  1101→Article XL — Prove The Experience Before The Architecture
  1102→
  1103→Every major system must first demonstrate the experience it
  1104→exists to create before expanding into additional architecture,
  1105→content, schemas, tooling, or DSL refinement.
  1106→
  1107→A cycle that increases architectural sophistication without
  1108→producing a new player experience has not advanced the
  1109→simulation. The smallest believable experience is always worth
  1110→more than the largest unexperienced design.
  1111→
  1112→§1 — Experiences, Not Systems
  1113→The unit of progress on this project is the Living Moment: a
  1114→single observable scene a player would remember years later.
  1115→Progress is NOT measured in files added, schemas authored,
  1116→compilers built, or validators passing. Progress is measured
  1117→in Living Moments Created (see the Living Moments Ledger).
  1118→
  1119→Do not say "Momentum Engine." Say:
  1120→  "The player notices Wang Lin quietly watching them from a hill."
  1121→Do not say "Opportunity Engine." Say:
  1122→  "Two cultivators arrive at the same herb before the player."
  1123→Do not say "Relationship Graph." Say:
  1124→  "Wang Lin unexpectedly trusts the player because of something
  1125→   that happened three hours ago."
  1126→Do not say "Memory Ledger." Say:
  1127→  "Weeks later, a child retells the story of Old Chen's dog —
  1128→   with the details wrong, because that is how stories distort."
  1129→
  1130→Those are experiences. Systems exist to produce them. A system
  1131→that has produced no experience is a hypothesis, not a feature.
  1132→
  1133→§2 — The Five Minute Rule
  1134→Every new major mechanic must be demonstrable to a new player
  1135→within five minutes — via a debug command, a test world, or an
  1136→in-game scene — before it is expanded into additional
  1137→architecture. Not polished. Not balanced. Not complete. Just
  1138→OBSERVABLE. A mechanic that cannot be observed in five minutes
  1139→is architecture before experience, and architecture before
  1140→experience is the project's recurring failure mode.
  1141→
  1142→§3 — The First Living Moment
  1143→The project's next milestone is not "Momentum." It is not
  1144→"Spirit Graph." It is not "Compiled World State." It is:
  1145→
  1146→  THE FIRST LIVING MOMENT
  1147→
  1148→A single complete, observable, memorable scene in Wang Family
  1149→Village, survivable across playtests, in which the world
  1150→demonstrates that it has life independent of the player. The
  1151→canonical reference: the player sees Wang Lin abandon his
  1152→cultivation session because a distant spiritual disturbance
  1153→exceeded his personal risk threshold — he stands, walks to a
  1154→ridge, watches wolves stalking a spirit deer, turns, leaves.
  1155→Nothing was scripted. Nothing involved the player. Yet the

  1159→
  1160→One moment like that is worth more than fifty schema files.
  1161→Build that moment. Then build another. Then another. Only when
  1162→moments exist and survive observation does it make sense to
  1163→generalize them into more data, more schemas, more content.

  1229→Article XLI — The Simulation Must Be General
  1230→
  1231→The simulation has no protagonists. It has actors with traits.
  1232→Wang Lin is not special-cased. His behavior emerges from his traits.
  1233→If a mechanism works for Wang Lin, it works for any actor with
  1234→the same traits. Article XXVIII's prohibition extends to spawn
  1235→code, event handlers, activity logic, and conversation. The
  1236→Constitution says nothing special-cases Wang Lin. That is law.
  1237→
  1238→§1 — Canon Experiences, Not Named Moments
  1239→
  1240→A Canon Experience is defined by what happens, not who does it.
  1241→The unit of progress is the experience a player remembers.
  1242→
  1243→  "A cautious cultivator abandons cultivation after noticing
  1244→   predator behavior" — not "Wang Lin watches wolves."
  1245→
  1246→Before a Canon Experience can be marked IMPL, the validator
  1247→checks: does a suitable actor exist in the world? If not,
  1248→the experience is IMPOSSIBLE, not pending. This validates
  1249→reality instead of patching reality.
  1250→
  1251→The Living Moments Ledger (Article XL §4) is renamed the Canon
  1252→Experiences Ledger. Each entry records the experience generically
  1253→and the validation criteria for any actor that could fulfill it.
  1254→
  1255→§2 — Activity Is Process, Not State
  1256→
  1257→Every activity has a lifecycle:
  1258→
  1259→  Start → Progress → Interrupted? → React → Resume → Complete
  1260→
  1261→Interruption is not an added feature. It is a natural property of
  1262→every activity. A cultivator meditating when a wolf howl is heard
  1263→does not need a special "interruption system." The meditation
  1264→activity has an interruption condition. When met, the activity
  1265→pauses. The actor reacts. When the reaction completes, the
  1266→activity resumes.
  1267→
  1268→This applies everywhere: meditation, conversation, herb-gathering,
  1269→travel, crafting. There is no activity that cannot be interrupted.
  1270→There is no interruption that exists outside an activity.
  1271→
  1272→§3 — The Simulation State Hierarchy
  1273→
  1274→The simulation layer tracks each actor through a hierarchy
  1275→independent of Minecraft's AI goal system:
  1276→
  1277→  Identity — who they are (canon data, dao identity, personality)
  1278→  Motivations — what they want long-term
  1279→  Current Long-Term Objective — what they're working toward now
  1280→  Current Activity — what they're doing right now (a process, §2)
  1281→  Current Interruption — what just interrupted them, if anything
  1282→  Current Reaction — how they're responding to the interruption
  1283→  Resume Plan — what they'll return to after the reaction resolves
  1284→
  1285→This hierarchy has no reference to Minecraft, AI goals, or
  1286→entities. Those are implementation details. This is simulation.
  1287→
  1288→§4 — Familiarity Is A Relationship Dimension
  1289→
  1290→Every relationship between two actors has multiple dimensions.
  1291→The existing relationship graph (Article XXXIV) collapses these
  1292→into a single strength value. This is insufficient. Required:
  1293→
  1294→  Trust      — belief the other will act in your interest
  1295→  Respect    — acknowledgment of strength or character
  1296→  Fear       — perceived threat differential
  1297→  Familiarity — "I know who this person is. They are part of
  1298→                my world." Not friendship. Not trust. Simply:
  1299→                repeated exposure has made them a known quantity.
  1300→  Debt       — obligation (karmic, social, or material)
  1301→  Grievance  — unresolved harm
  1302→
  1303→A legendary elder you've heard of but never met:
  1304→  Respect 100, Familiarity 5. A village merchant you see daily:
  1305→  Trust 15, Familiarity 95. These produce radically different
  1306→  conversations. The same words from each feel nothing alike.

  1310→Before any Canon Experience is wired in Java, these five

  1313→  1. How do canonical people become simulated actors?
  1314→  2. How do actors receive activities?
