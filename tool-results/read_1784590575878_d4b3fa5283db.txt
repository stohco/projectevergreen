   401→   401→    - Will it harm them?
   402→   402→    - Why do they want it?
   403→   403→    - Have they demonstrated judgment?
   404→   404→    - Is this the right time?
   405→   405→  Then his True Body creates a copy, and a clone hands it over.
   406→   406→
   407→   407→This is the experience of being Wang Lin's acquaintance in
   408→   408→Renegade Immortal. The world does not transact with him.
   409→   409→It is judged by him, and occasionally, rewarded.
   410→   410→
   411→   411→§ XXXI.3 — The Stronger Metric for Living Chapter 1
   412→   412→The metric for Chapter 1 (Wang Family Village) is not
   413→   413→"Can the player spend two hours there?"
   414→   414→It is:
   415→   415→
   416→   416→  "Could somebody who never read Renegade Immortal
   417→   417→   accidentally recreate Wang Lin's early life?"
   418→   418→
   419→   419→This is a much stronger bar. The player should be able to live
   420→   420→through the events that shaped Wang Lin — the wandering
   421→   421→cultivator's arrival, the rumors, the disappearances, the
   422→   422→village elder's worry, the strange jade, the silent birds,
   423→   423→the restless wolves — without any quest marker, tutorial,
   424→   424→or narrator telling them what is happening.
   425→   425→
   426→   426→Nobody gives a quest. Everything simply happens.
   427→   427→Curiosity, not instruction, drives progression.
   428→   428→
   429→   429→§ XXXI.4 — The Gold-Standard Location Template
   430→   430→Every location must eventually satisfy this template.
   431→   431→Wang Family Village is the first to do so, and becomes the
   432→   432→canonical reference implementation for every future village,
   433→   433→sect, city, cave, and continent.
   434→   434→
   435→   435→  1.  Ecology     — food chain, seasons, spirit veins, resources
   436→   436→  2.  Society     — NPC relationships, routines, disputes, celebrations
   437→   437→  3.  Economy     — production, trade, shortages, prices
   438→   438→  4.  History     — persistent consequences and remembered events
   439→   439→  5.  Opportunities — dynamic events that emerge naturally
   440→   440→  6.  Affordances — unique interactions with every important NPC and object
   441→   441→  7.  Mentorship  — NPCs can teach, challenge, recruit, request, reward
   442→   442→  8.  Conflict    — factions, rivalries, ambitions, hidden agendas
   443→   443→  9.  Exploration — secrets discoverable through observation, not markers
   444→   444→  10. Evolution   — the location changes over time whether or not
   445→   445→                    the player is present
   446→   446→
   447→   447→A location missing any of these ten dimensions is not complete.
   448→   448→Wang Family Village must pass all ten before Chapter 2 begins.
   449→   449→
   450→   450→§ XXXI.5 — The Memory Metric (Addendum to Living Chapters)
   451→   451→A Living Chapter is complete not only when the player can live
   452→   452→through it without a quest log, but also when:
   453→   453→
   454→   454→  THE WORLD REMEMBERS.
   455→   455→
   456→   456→A village that resets when the player leaves is a diorama.
   457→   457→A village that carries forward what happened — the dog that
   458→   458→died in the wolf attack, the elder who no longer mentions it
   459→   459→but whose routines shifted, the child who retells the story
   460→   460→months later — is a place that has history.
   461→   461→
   462→   462→Memory is persistence with weight. Every significant event in
   463→   463→a Living Chapter must be capable of being remembered,
   464→   464→forgotten, misremembered, or retold by the NPCs who witnessed
   465→   465→it. The canonical test:
   466→   466→
   467→   467→  Week one — Old Chen owns a dog.
   468→   468→  Week three — wolf attack — the dog dies.
   469→   469→  Week five — Old Chen no longer mentions the dog.
   470→   470→              Instead he says, "Used to have one."
   471→   471→  Months later — a child tells the story.
   472→   472→
   473→   473→When that sequence can occur without any developer scripting
   474→   474→the specific beat, the world has memory. Until then, it does not.

   477→   477→THE CONVERSATION & MOTIVATION AMENDMENTS (Articles XXXII–XXXIV)
   478→   478→Added when the project recognized that conversation is not a
   479→   479→menu system layered over the world — it IS the world. And that
   480→   480→"desire" was too narrow a word for what actually drives an
   481→   481→Er Gen character. Wang Lin does not chase desires. He responds
   482→   482→to necessity, protects what matters, honors debts, pursues
   483→   483→long-term goals, and occasionally acts out of compassion or
   484→   484→vengeance. The simulation must reflect that richness.
   485→   485→═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
   486→   486→
   487→   487→Article XXXII — Conversation Is The World
   488→   488→Dialogue is not a separate menu system. Dialogue is a simulation
   489→   489→system, governed by the same reasoning engine as cultivation,
   490→   490→combat, and exploration. The Skyrim problem — "Hello. Tell me
   491→   491→about... Goodbye." over and over — is forbidden.
   492→   492→
   493→   493→§ XXXII.1 — Three Conversation Modes
   494→   494→There are exactly three conversation modes. The system selects
   495→   495→the mode based on context, never on a toggle.
   496→   496→
   497→   497→  1. AMBIENT (≈90% of interactions) — No pause. No UI takeover.
   498→   498→     The player right-clicks an NPC. The NPC glances over and
   499→   499→     HOLDS STILL — pausing their pathing/schedule as a courtesy
   500→   500→     so the player can explore the radial verb-prompts at their
   501→   501→     own pace and talk as much as they want without the NPC
   502→   502→     wandering off. "...Yes?" Radial verb-prompts appear. BUT
   503→   503→     THE WORLD KEEPS RUNNING. Birds fly. OTHER NPCs walk.
   504→   504→     Children run by. Li Muwan walks past carrying herbs. The
   505→   505→     sun moves. Rain falls. Only the NPC you are talking to
   506→   506→     holds still for you; everything else is alive. The player
   507→   507→     remains mobile (may walk-and-talk, see § XXXII.6). When the
   508→   508→     player selects "Nevermind" / dismisses the conversation,
   509→   509→     the NPC resumes their schedule and pathing from where they
   510→   510→     paused. This is the default.
   511→   511→
   512→   512→  2. FOCUS — When the conversation actually matters (discussing
   513→   513→     Dao, requesting a Heaven Defying Bead copy, entering a sect,
   514→   514→     negotiating). The camera subtly zooms. Player movement
   515→   515→     disables. The NPC holds still (as in Ambient). BUT THE
   516→   516→     WORLD KEEPS RUNNING. Other NPCs still move. The sun still
   517→   517→     sets. Rain still falls. Rumors continue spreading. Only
   518→   518→     the player stops moving because the player is busy talking.
   519→   519→     Exactly like real life.
   520→   520→
   521→   521→  3. MAJOR CANON MOMENTS — Very rare. Inheritance, tribulation,
   522→   522→     True Body meeting, major revelations. Cinematic framing —
   523→   523→     camera, music, animations, special dialogue. These must be
   524→   524→     incredibly uncommon. Otherwise they lose impact.
   525→   525→
   526→   526→§ XXXII.1a — "Holds Still" Is A Courtesy, Not A Freeze
   527→   527→When an NPC holds still for the player, it is a behavioral
   528→   528→courtesy, not a simulation freeze. The NPC is still perceiving
   529→   529→the world: if a threat appears, they react (§ XXXII.5). If
   530→   530→their schedule has a hard time-bound event (a sect lecture
   531→   531→they must attend, a tribulation window), they may break the
   532→   532→courtesy: "I have to go." and resume pathing. The courtesy
   533→   533→expires on (a) player dismiss ("Nevermind"), (b) NPC
   534→   534→hard-deadline, or (c) world interruption (combat, disaster).
   535→   535→It does NOT expire on idle — the NPC will wait patiently
   536→   536→because that is what a person does when someone is talking
   537→   537→to them.
   538→   538→
   539→   539→§ XXXII.2 — Conversation Affordances, Not Dialogue Trees
   540→   540→The interface shows VERBS, not dialogue options. Not "Tell me
   541→   541→about yourself. Where are you from? Goodbye." Instead:
   542→   542→Discuss, Request, Teach Me, Offer, Trade, Travel Together,
   543→   543→Observe Technique, Challenge, Give Gift, Ask Opinion, Ask About...
   544→   544→
   545→   545→Each verb expands per-NPC. The same verb produces entirely
   546→   546→different sub-menus for different NPCs:
   547→   547→  Wang Lin → Request → {Technique, Artifact Copy, Advice,
   548→   548→                         Restriction, Memory, Training}
   549→   549→  Li Muwan → Request → {Pill, Herb Identification, Medical
   550→   550→                         Advice, Alchemy Lesson}
   551→   551→  Xu Liguo → Request → {Scout, Possess, Steal, Distract,
   552→   552→                         Cause Trouble}
   553→   553→Same system. Entirely different UI. No special-casing — the
   554→   554→affordances are data the UI discovers (Article XXVIII).
   555→   555→
   556→   556→§ XXXII.3 — Requests Are Simulations, Not Transactions
   557→   557→Asking Wang Lin for the God Slaying Sword does not instantly
   558→   558→receive it. Wang Lin THINKS. He evaluates relationship, current
   559→   559→cultivation, knowledge, judgment, intent, history. Then:
   560→   560→  "..." / "No." / "You wouldn't survive using it."
   561→   561→  OR "I can. But not yet. Learn this first." (hands something else)
   562→   562→  OR "...Very well." (True Body manufactures a copy, clone
   563→   563→      retrieves it, hands it over)
   564→   564→  OR "If you still want it after Mosquito Valley..." (a challenge
   565→   565→      begins naturally, no quest log)
   566→   566→
   567→   567→§ XXXII.4 — Fuzzy Asking
   568→   568→The player does not select from item IDs. The player types or
   569→   569→selects intent: "I want something that helps fight souls." The
   570→   570→NPC REASONS about the intent — not a database search — and
   571→   571→chooses: maybe a Soul Banner, maybe a Soul Restriction, maybe
   572→   572→"Not yet." The NPC's response reflects their judgment of what
   573→   573→the player needs, not what the player asked for verbatim.
   574→   574→
   575→   575→§ XXXII.5 — Conversations Are Interruptible
   576→   576→If something happens — thunder, sect attack, spirit beast,
   577→   577→tribulation, explosion, enemy arrives — NPCs react. Wang Lin
   578→   578→stops talking mid-sentence. "Later." Draws his weapon. The
   579→   579→conversation ends naturally because the world kept moving.
   580→   580→The player never entered a frozen dialogue world.
   581→   581→
   582→   582→§ XXXII.6 — Walk And Talk
   583→   583→NPCs do not stop to converse. Wang Lin says "Come." and walks
   584→   584→toward a mountain. The player walks beside him. The conversation
   585→   585→UI stays open in the corner. Both move through the world. He
   586→   586→points out a restriction on a cliff. A spirit beast appears.
   587→   587→The conversation naturally pauses while dealing with it. Then
   588→   588→resumes. This is how conversations happen in Renegade Immortal
   589→   589→— not standing face-to-face in a frozen box.
   590→   590→
   591→   591→§ XXXII.7 — Personality Changes The Interface
   592→   592→The same conversation system renders differently per-NPC:
   593→   593→  Wang Lin — very few words, lots of silence, observes you,
   594→   594→              long pauses before responding.
   595→   595→  Xu Liguo — interrupts constantly, makes jokes, changes
   596→   596→              topics, lies.
   597→   597→  Li Muwan — detailed, patient, explains thoroughly.
   598→   598→  Situ Nan — laughs, mocks, walks away mid-conversation.
   599→   599→  Qing Shui — may answer "...". For thirty seconds.
   600→   600→This is personality through interaction, not just dialogue text.
   601→   601→
   602→   602→§ XXXII.8 — Conversations Are Stateful
   603→   603→If the player asked Wang Lin about restrictions yesterday, he
   604→   604→remembers. If he promised to teach after Foundation Establishment,
   605→   605→that option is unavailable until then. If he refused the God
   606→   606→Slaying Sword request because the player wasn't ready, he won't
   607→   607→pretend it's the first time. Conversations evolve as relationship,
   608→   608→reputation, knowledge, and cultivation change.
   609→   609→
   610→   610→§ XXXII.9 — Conversations Are World Events
   611→   611→Dialogue is not outside the world. A conversation is an event
   612→   612→with a location, volume, and witnesses. Nearby NPCs overhear.
   613→   613→Beliefs update. Rumors may form. Relationships change. History
   614→   614→records it. Loudly asking Wang Lin about the Heaven Defying Bead
   615→   615→in the middle of Heng Yue Sect is potentially dangerous — someone
   616→   616→may overhear fragments, grow suspicious, follow the player. A
   617→   617→private discussion in a secluded cave has different consequences.
   618→   618→The location of a conversation is a gameplay variable.
   619→   619→
   620→   620→Article XXXIII — Motivations, Not Desires
   621→   621→Article XXXI.1 named the per-cycle NPC state "desire." That word
   622→   622→is too narrow. Many interactions are motivated by things that are
   623→   623→not desires at all: fears, duties, debts, grudges, survival.
   624→   624→
   625→   625→The per-cycle NPC state is hereby renamed MOTIVATION STATE.
   626→   626→The ten social engines remain (Art XXXI.1) — they are the
   627→   627→expression layer. What feeds them is the motivation state,
   628→   628→which draws from a richer vocabulary of DRIVING FORCES:
   629→   629→
   630→   630→  ambitions, fears, duties, debts, promises, grudges,
   631→   631→  curiosity, love, loyalty, survival, greed, vengeance,
   632→   632→  sect orders, cultivation bottlenecks, opportunities.
   633→   633→
   634→   634→§ XXXIII.1 — Every Interaction Must Have A Reason
   635→   635→Nobody wakes up wanting to talk to the player. Everyone wakes
   636→   636→with problems, ambitions, fears, obligations, loyalties, and
   637→   637→opportunities. Then they ask:
   638→   638→  "Who, if anyone, can help me?"
   639→   639→  "Who is standing in my way?"
   640→   640→The player MIGHT be the answer. The player might not. The player
   641→   641→is relevant only when circumstances make them relevant.
   642→   642→
   643→   643→§ XXXIII.2 — The Player Is Not Default Relevant
   644→   644→Wang Lin's early priorities: protect family, become stronger,
   645→   645→avoid attracting attention, acquire resources, study restrictions.
   646→   646→Notice what is missing: the player. Until the player becomes
   647→   647→relevant — by demonstrating a skill, owing a debt, holding a
   648→   648→grudge, or standing in the way — Wang Lin has no motivation
   649→   649→directed at the player. This is not coldness. This is how Er Gen
   650→   650→characters actually behave. Relevance is earned, not assumed.
   651→   651→
   652→   652→§ XXXIII.3 — Relevance Is Earned Through Circumstance
   653→   653→The player becomes relevant when an NPC's driving force intersects
   654→   654→with the player's demonstrated behavior:
   655→   655→  - Player excellent with restrictions + Wang Lin needs help
   656→   656→    deciphering a restriction → player becomes relevant.
   657→   657→  - Player knows herbs + Li Muwan needs Crimson Root → player
   658→   658→    becomes relevant.
   659→   659→  - Player helped Wang Lin + Wang Lin owes a favor → player
   660→   660→    becomes relevant (Wang Lin may seek the player out).
   661→   661→  - Player stole from merchant + merchant has a grievance →
   662→   662→    player becomes relevant (merchant may warn others, refuse
   663→   663→    trade, or set a trap).
   664→   664→Relevance is never granted. It is earned or incurred.
   665→   665→
   666→   666→Article XXXIV — Relationships Are Graphs
   667→   667→A relationship is not a number. A relationship is a graph of
   668→   668→distinct, independently-variable dimensions. "Friendship > 50"
   669→   669→is forbidden. NPCs maintain structured relationship state.
   670→   670→
   671→   671→§ XXXIV.1 — The Relationship Graph
   672→   672→Every NPC maintains a per-target relationship graph with at
   673→   673→minimum:
   674→   674→  Trust (0-100)        — does the NPC believe the target's words
   675→   675→  Respect (0-100)      — does the NPC esteem the target's capability
   676→   676→  Fear (0-100)         — does the NPC fear the target
   677→   677→  Debt (signed int)    — outstanding favors owed (+ = NPC owes
   678→   678→                         target, - = target owes NPC)
   679→   679→  Grievance (0-100)    — accumulated resentment from wrongs
   680→   680→  Shared History (int) — count of significant shared events
   681→   681→  Known Skills [list]  — skills the NPC has observed the target use
   682→   682→  Known Personality [list] — traits the NPC has inferred
   683→   683→                         (e.g. "keeps promises", "doesn't betray
   684→   684→                         allies", "greedy", "merciful")
   685→   685→
   686→   686→Two NPCs can have completely different graphs for the same
   687→   687→player. The village elder's graph (Trust 82, Respect 71, Fear
   688→   688→10, Grievance 0) is a different relationship than Teng
   689→   689→Huayuan's graph (Trust 5, Fear 88, Grievance 64, Respect 91).
   690→   690→The simulation treats them as different relationships because
   691→   691→they ARE different relationships.
   692→   692→
   693→   693→§ XXXIV.2 — Favors Are Callable Debts
   694→   694→A favor is not "relationship += 20." A favor is a structured
   695→   695→debt that persists and can be called in later:
   696→   696→  Owner: who did the favor
   697→   697→  Recipient: who received the benefit
   698→   698→  Magnitude: Minor | Moderate | Major | Life-Saving
   699→   699→  Reason: what happened
   700→   700→  Expires: when (Never for Life-Saving)
   701→   701→  Callable: can the owner demand repayment
   702→   702→  Called: has it been called (and was it honored)
   703→   703→
   704→   704→Wang Lin once saved the player from a Core Formation cultivator.
   705→   705→That is a Major favor, never expires. Years later, the player
   706→   706→asks to study the Ji Realm. Wang Lin reasons:
   707→   707→  "Player once risked his life for me. Player has shown
   708→   708→   restraint. Player has sufficient cultivation. Teaching
   709→   709→   benefits him. I owe him. Yes."
   710→   710→The favor is called. The debt is honored. This feels earned
   711→   711→because it IS earned — the simulation tracked the favor for
   712→   712→years and surfaced it at the right moment.
   713→   713→
   714→   714→§ XXXIV.3 — Grievances Persist And Spread
   715→   715→A grievance is not forgotten when the player leaves the area.
   716→   716→If the player steals a spirit stone, the merchant remembers.
   717→   717→The village remembers. A rumor spreads. People become cautious.
   718→   718→Li Muwan may still trust the player (her graph is independent),
   719→   719→but the merchant does not. Grievances feed the RumorEngine
   720→   720→(Art XXXI.1) and shape future interactions. A grievance may
   721→   721→decay if amends are made, but it never silently resets.
   722→   722→
   723→   723→§ XXXIV.4 — The Deconfliction Principle
   724→   724→When multiple NPCs have motivations that could target the
   725→   725→player simultaneously, they do NOT all approach. Each reasons
   726→   726→independently:
   727→   727→  NPC A: "Player is busy. Tomorrow."
   728→   728→  NPC B: "The merchant can help instead. I'll ask him."
   729→   729→  NPC C: "I'll gather the herbs myself."
   730→   730→  NPC D: "I'll ask Wang Lin."
   731→   731→  NPC E: "Not urgent."
   732→   732→  NPC F: "Player owes me. I'll go now."
   733→   733→Only NPC F actually approaches. The others resolved their
   734→   734→motivation through alternative means. This is the "everyone
   735→   735→lining up" problem, solved by the same reasoning engine that
   736→   736→governs every other NPC decision. The player is never mobbed.
   737→   737→

   739→   739→THE CHARACTER-FIRST & UNIVERSAL INTERACTION AMENDMENTS
   740→   740→(Articles XXXV–XXXVIII)
   741→   741→Added when the project recognized that even motivation-driven
   742→   742→simulation is still simulation-first. The world should not run
   743→   743→on motivations. It should run on people. Wang Lin should not
   744→   744→wake up and ask "which motivation has the highest score?" He
   745→   745→should ask "What do I believe is happening? What kind of person
   746→   746→am I? What matters today? What options do I actually have?"
   747→   747→And the common language through which every entity — player,
   748→   748→NPC, beast, artifact, sect, location — engages the world is a
   749→   749→single universal object: the Interaction.
   750→   750→═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
   751→   751→
   752→   752→Article XXXV — Character-First, Not Simulation-First
   753→   753→The world does not run on motivations. The world runs on people.
   754→   754→Motivation is one ingredient in a character's reasoning, not
   755→   755→the driver of it. The old pipeline (Motivation → Interaction)
   756→   756→is superseded.
   757→   757→
   758→   758→The character reasoning pipeline is:
   759→   759→  Perception → Beliefs → Relationships → Identity →
   760→   760→  Current Circumstances → Opportunities → Prediction →
   761→   761→  Intent → Plan → Action
   762→   762→
   763→   763→§ XXXV.1 — What A Character Asks Each Cycle
   764→   764→Every character, each reasoning cycle, asks:
   765→   765→  - What do I believe is happening? (Perception → Beliefs)
   766→   766→  - What kind of person am I? (Identity)
   767→   767→  - What matters today? (Identity + Circumstances)
   768→   768→  - What options do I actually have? (Opportunities)
   769→   769→  - What do I predict will happen if I act? (Prediction)
   770→   770→  - What will I attempt? (Intent → Plan → Action)
   771→   771→
   772→   772→Motivation is one input to Intent — alongside beliefs,
   773→   773→relationships, identity, circumstances, and prediction.
   774→   774→It does not disappear, but it is no longer the apex.
   775→   775→
   776→   776→§ XXXV.2 — Relevance Is Universal, Not Player-Special
   777→   777→The old `player_relevance` field (Article XXXIII) special-cased
   778→   778→the player. That is forbidden. Relevance is calculated for EVERY
   779→   779→actor a character can perceive, using the same system:
   780→   780→  relevance(target) = f(perceived_threat, perceived_opportunity,
   781→   781→                       shared_history, current_goals_alignment,
   782→   782→                       power_differential, recent_interactions)
   783→   783→
   784→   784→Wang Lin calculates relevance for Li Muwan, Situ Nan, Teng
   785→   785→Huayuan, a random disciple, and the player using the same
   786→   786→function. The player is a variable, not a special case. This
   787→   787→makes the system extensible to the entire Er Gen multiverse
   788→   788→without per-player code paths.
   789→   789→
   790→   790→§ XXXV.3 — Understanding Is A Relationship Dimension
   791→   791→The relationship graph (Article XXXIV) is extended with a new
   792→   792→dimension: Understanding (0-100). This is NOT Respect — you can
   793→   793→respect someone you do not understand, and understand someone
   794→   794→you do not respect.
   795→   795→  - Wang Lin trusts Situ Nan but does not always understand him.
   796→   796→  - Li Muwan understands Wang Lin extremely well.
   797→   797→  - Xu Liguo misunderstands everyone constantly.
   798→   798→Understanding shapes dialogue: low understanding means the NPC
   799→   799→misinterprets the player's intent, responds to what they THOUGHT
   800→   800→was said, and may agree to the wrong thing or refuse the right
   801→   801→one. High understanding means the NPC reads subtext and responds
   802→   802→to what the player meant, not just what they said.
   803→   803→
   804→   804→Article XXXVI — The Universal Interaction
   805→   805→There is one universal object through which every entity in the
   806→   806→game engages every other entity: the INTERACTION. Every NPC,
   807→   807→item, beast, plant, technique, formation, location, artifact,
   808→   808→and sect exposes a set of interactions. The player, NPCs, beasts,
   809→   809→and even the world itself initiate interactions. The affordance
   810→   810→system (Article XXVIII) and the social engines (Article XXXI.1)
   811→   811→are now unified under this single object.
   812→   812→
   813→   813→§ XXXVI.1 — What Is An Interaction
   814→   814→An interaction is a bidirectional engagement between two entities
   815→   815→(initiator and target) with: a verb, an intent, a context, a
   816→   816→negotiation, and an outcome. It is not a quest, not a dialogue
   817→   817→option, not a transaction. It is the atomic unit of all
   818→   818→engagement in the world.
   819→   819→
   820→   820→§ XXXVI.2 — The Interaction Catalog
   821→   821→Every entity exposes interactions from a shared catalog. The
   822→   822→catalog includes but is not limited to:

   856→   856→Article XXXVII — The Conversation UI (Three Layers + Learning)
   857→   857→The conversation UI must solve the hardest design problem in the
   858→   858→project: the player does not know what they don't know. Showing
   859→   859→every possible interaction spoils discovery. Making everything
   860→   860→free-form typing leaves 99% of players lost. The answer is
   861→   861→neither — it is three layers, with a UI that learns.
   862→   862→
   863→   863→§ XXXVII.1 — Layer 1: Obvious Actions
   864→   864→When the player right-clicks an NPC, a small clean verb wheel

   868→   868→yet know those are possibilities. The wheel is small enough to
   869→   869→read in a glance and clean enough to feel like talking to a
   870→   870→person, not browsing a database.

   881→   881→§ XXXVII.3 — Layer 3: Natural Language
