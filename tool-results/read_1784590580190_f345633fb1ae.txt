    87‚Üí   487‚Üí   487‚ÜíArticle XXXII ‚Äî Conversation Is The World
    88‚Üí   488‚Üí   488‚ÜíDialogue is not a separate menu system. Dialogue is a simulation
    89‚Üí   489‚Üí   489‚Üísystem, governed by the same reasoning engine as cultivation,
    90‚Üí   490‚Üí   490‚Üícombat, and exploration. The Skyrim problem ‚Äî "Hello. Tell me
    91‚Üí   491‚Üí   491‚Üíabout... Goodbye." over and over ‚Äî is forbidden.
    92‚Üí   492‚Üí   492‚Üí
    93‚Üí   493‚Üí   493‚Üí¬ß XXXII.1 ‚Äî Three Conversation Modes
    94‚Üí   494‚Üí   494‚ÜíThere are exactly three conversation modes. The system selects
    95‚Üí   495‚Üí   495‚Üíthe mode based on context, never on a toggle.
    96‚Üí   496‚Üí   496‚Üí
    97‚Üí   497‚Üí   497‚Üí  1. AMBIENT (‚âà90% of interactions) ‚Äî No pause. No UI takeover.
    98‚Üí   498‚Üí   498‚Üí     The player right-clicks an NPC. The NPC glances over and
    99‚Üí   499‚Üí   499‚Üí     HOLDS STILL ‚Äî pausing their pathing/schedule as a courtesy
   100‚Üí   500‚Üí   500‚Üí     so the player can explore the radial verb-prompts at their
   101‚Üí   501‚Üí   501‚Üí     own pace and talk as much as they want without the NPC
   102‚Üí   502‚Üí   502‚Üí     wandering off. "...Yes?" Radial verb-prompts appear. BUT
   103‚Üí   503‚Üí   503‚Üí     THE WORLD KEEPS RUNNING. Birds fly. OTHER NPCs walk.
   104‚Üí   504‚Üí   504‚Üí     Children run by. Li Muwan walks past carrying herbs. The
   105‚Üí   505‚Üí   505‚Üí     sun moves. Rain falls. Only the NPC you are talking to
   106‚Üí   506‚Üí   506‚Üí     holds still for you; everything else is alive. The player
   107‚Üí   507‚Üí   507‚Üí     remains mobile (may walk-and-talk, see ¬ß XXXII.6). When the
   108‚Üí   508‚Üí   508‚Üí     player selects "Nevermind" / dismisses the conversation,
   109‚Üí   509‚Üí   509‚Üí     the NPC resumes their schedule and pathing from where they
   110‚Üí   510‚Üí   510‚Üí     paused. This is the default.
   111‚Üí   511‚Üí   511‚Üí
   112‚Üí   512‚Üí   512‚Üí  2. FOCUS ‚Äî When the conversation actually matters (discussing
   113‚Üí   513‚Üí   513‚Üí     Dao, requesting a Heaven Defying Bead copy, entering a sect,
   114‚Üí   514‚Üí   514‚Üí     negotiating). The camera subtly zooms. Player movement
   115‚Üí   515‚Üí   515‚Üí     disables. The NPC holds still (as in Ambient). BUT THE
   116‚Üí   516‚Üí   516‚Üí     WORLD KEEPS RUNNING. Other NPCs still move. The sun still
   117‚Üí   517‚Üí   517‚Üí     sets. Rain still falls. Rumors continue spreading. Only
   118‚Üí   518‚Üí   518‚Üí     the player stops moving because the player is busy talking.
   119‚Üí   519‚Üí   519‚Üí     Exactly like real life.
   120‚Üí   520‚Üí   520‚Üí
   121‚Üí   521‚Üí   521‚Üí  3. MAJOR CANON MOMENTS ‚Äî Very rare. Inheritance, tribulation,
   122‚Üí   522‚Üí   522‚Üí     True Body meeting, major revelations. Cinematic framing ‚Äî
   123‚Üí   523‚Üí   523‚Üí     camera, music, animations, special dialogue. These must be
   124‚Üí   524‚Üí   524‚Üí     incredibly uncommon. Otherwise they lose impact.
   125‚Üí   525‚Üí   525‚Üí
   126‚Üí   526‚Üí   526‚Üí¬ß XXXII.1a ‚Äî "Holds Still" Is A Courtesy, Not A Freeze
   127‚Üí   527‚Üí   527‚ÜíWhen an NPC holds still for the player, it is a behavioral
   128‚Üí   528‚Üí   528‚Üícourtesy, not a simulation freeze. The NPC is still perceiving
   129‚Üí   529‚Üí   529‚Üíthe world: if a threat appears, they react (¬ß XXXII.5). If
   130‚Üí   530‚Üí   530‚Üítheir schedule has a hard time-bound event (a sect lecture
   131‚Üí   531‚Üí   531‚Üíthey must attend, a tribulation window), they may break the
   132‚Üí   532‚Üí   532‚Üícourtesy: "I have to go." and resume pathing. The courtesy
   133‚Üí   533‚Üí   533‚Üíexpires on (a) player dismiss ("Nevermind"), (b) NPC
   134‚Üí   534‚Üí   534‚Üíhard-deadline, or (c) world interruption (combat, disaster).
   135‚Üí   535‚Üí   535‚ÜíIt does NOT expire on idle ‚Äî the NPC will wait patiently
   136‚Üí   536‚Üí   536‚Üíbecause that is what a person does when someone is talking
   137‚Üí   537‚Üí   537‚Üíto them.
   138‚Üí   538‚Üí   538‚Üí
   139‚Üí   539‚Üí   539‚Üí¬ß XXXII.2 ‚Äî Conversation Affordances, Not Dialogue Trees

   142‚Üí   542‚Üí   542‚ÜíDiscuss, Request, Teach Me, Offer, Trade, Travel Together,

   144‚Üí   544‚Üí   544‚Üí
   145‚Üí   545‚Üí   545‚ÜíEach verb expands per-NPC. The same verb produces entirely
   146‚Üí   546‚Üí   546‚Üídifferent sub-menus for different NPCs:
   147‚Üí   547‚Üí   547‚Üí  Wang Lin ‚Üí Request ‚Üí {Technique, Artifact Copy, Advice,
   148‚Üí   548‚Üí   548‚Üí                         Restriction, Memory, Training}
   149‚Üí   549‚Üí   549‚Üí  Li Muwan ‚Üí Request ‚Üí {Pill, Herb Identification, Medical

   153‚Üí   553‚Üí   553‚ÜíSame system. Entirely different UI. No special-casing ‚Äî the

   155‚Üí   555‚Üí   555‚Üí
   156‚Üí   556‚Üí   556‚Üí¬ß XXXII.3 ‚Äî Requests Are Simulations, Not Transactions
   157‚Üí   557‚Üí   557‚ÜíAsking Wang Lin for the God Slaying Sword does not instantly
   158‚Üí   558‚Üí   558‚Üíreceive it. Wang Lin THINKS. He evaluates relationship, current

   160‚Üí   560‚Üí   560‚Üí  "..." / "No." / "You wouldn't survive using it."

   163‚Üí   563‚Üí   563‚Üí      retrieves it, hands it over)

   165‚Üí   565‚Üí   565‚Üí      begins naturally, no quest log)
   166‚Üí   566‚Üí   566‚Üí
   167‚Üí   567‚Üí   567‚Üí¬ß XXXII.4 ‚Äî Fuzzy Asking
   168‚Üí   568‚Üí   568‚ÜíThe player does not select from item IDs. The player types or
   169‚Üí   569‚Üí   569‚Üíselects intent: "I want something that helps fight souls." The
   170‚Üí   570‚Üí   570‚ÜíNPC REASONS about the intent ‚Äî not a database search ‚Äî and
   171‚Üí   571‚Üí   571‚Üíchooses: maybe a Soul Banner, maybe a Soul Restriction, maybe
   172‚Üí   572‚Üí   572‚Üí"Not yet." The NPC's response reflects their judgment of what
   173‚Üí   573‚Üí   573‚Üíthe player needs, not what the player asked for verbatim.
   174‚Üí   574‚Üí   574‚Üí
   175‚Üí   575‚Üí   575‚Üí¬ß XXXII.5 ‚Äî Conversations Are Interruptible
   176‚Üí   576‚Üí   576‚ÜíIf something happens ‚Äî thunder, sect attack, spirit beast,

   179‚Üí   579‚Üí   579‚Üíconversation ends naturally because the world kept moving.
   180‚Üí   580‚Üí   580‚ÜíThe player never entered a frozen dialogue world.
   181‚Üí   581‚Üí   581‚Üí
   182‚Üí   582‚Üí   582‚Üí¬ß XXXII.6 ‚Äî Walk And Talk

   184‚Üí   584‚Üí   584‚Üítoward a mountain. The player walks beside him. The conversation

   186‚Üí   586‚Üí   586‚Üípoints out a restriction on a cliff. A spirit beast appears.

   188‚Üí   588‚Üí   588‚Üíresumes. This is how conversations happen in Renegade Immortal
   189‚Üí   589‚Üí   589‚Üí‚Äî not standing face-to-face in a frozen box.
   190‚Üí   590‚Üí   590‚Üí
   191‚Üí   591‚Üí   591‚Üí¬ß XXXII.7 ‚Äî Personality Changes The Interface

   195‚Üí   595‚Üí   595‚Üí  Xu Liguo ‚Äî interrupts constantly, makes jokes, changes

   198‚Üí   598‚Üí   598‚Üí  Situ Nan ‚Äî laughs, mocks, walks away mid-conversation.
   199‚Üí   599‚Üí   599‚Üí  Qing Shui ‚Äî may answer "...". For thirty seconds.
   200‚Üí   600‚Üí   600‚ÜíThis is personality through interaction, not just dialogue text.
   201‚Üí   601‚Üí   601‚Üí
   202‚Üí   602‚Üí   602‚Üí¬ß XXXII.8 ‚Äî Conversations Are Stateful
   203‚Üí   603‚Üí   603‚ÜíIf the player asked Wang Lin about restrictions yesterday, he

   208‚Üí   608‚Üí   608‚Üíreputation, knowledge, and cultivation change.
   209‚Üí   609‚Üí   609‚Üí
   210‚Üí   610‚Üí   610‚Üí¬ß XXXII.9 ‚Äî Conversations Are World Events
   211‚Üí   611‚Üí   611‚ÜíDialogue is not outside the world. A conversation is an event
   212‚Üí   612‚Üí   612‚Üíwith a location, volume, and witnesses. Nearby NPCs overhear.
   213‚Üí   613‚Üí   613‚ÜíBeliefs update. Rumors may form. Relationships change. History
   214‚Üí   614‚Üí   614‚Üírecords it. Loudly asking Wang Lin about the Heaven Defying Bead
   215‚Üí   615‚Üí   615‚Üíin the middle of Heng Yue Sect is potentially dangerous ‚Äî someone
   216‚Üí   616‚Üí   616‚Üímay overhear fragments, grow suspicious, follow the player. A
   217‚Üí   617‚Üí   617‚Üíprivate discussion in a secluded cave has different consequences.
   218‚Üí   618‚Üí   618‚ÜíThe location of a conversation is a gameplay variable.
   219‚Üí   619‚Üí   619‚Üí
   220‚Üí   620‚Üí   620‚ÜíArticle XXXIII ‚Äî Motivations, Not Desires
   221‚Üí   621‚Üí   621‚ÜíArticle XXXI.1 named the per-cycle NPC state "desire." That word
   222‚Üí   622‚Üí   622‚Üíis too narrow. Many interactions are motivated by things that are
   223‚Üí   623‚Üí   623‚Üínot desires at all: fears, duties, debts, grudges, survival.
   224‚Üí   624‚Üí   624‚Üí
   225‚Üí   625‚Üí   625‚ÜíThe per-cycle NPC state is hereby renamed MOTIVATION STATE.
   226‚Üí   626‚Üí   626‚ÜíThe ten social engines remain (Art XXXI.1) ‚Äî they are the
   227‚Üí   627‚Üí   627‚Üíexpression layer. What feeds them is the motivation state,
   228‚Üí   628‚Üí   628‚Üíwhich draws from a richer vocabulary of DRIVING FORCES:
   229‚Üí   629‚Üí   629‚Üí
   230‚Üí   630‚Üí   630‚Üí  ambitions, fears, duties, debts, promises, grudges,
   231‚Üí   631‚Üí   631‚Üí  curiosity, love, loyalty, survival, greed, vengeance,
   232‚Üí   632‚Üí   632‚Üí  sect orders, cultivation bottlenecks, opportunities.
   233‚Üí   633‚Üí   633‚Üí
   234‚Üí   634‚Üí   634‚Üí¬ß XXXIII.1 ‚Äî Every Interaction Must Have A Reason
   235‚Üí   635‚Üí   635‚ÜíNobody wakes up wanting to talk to the player. Everyone wakes
   236‚Üí   636‚Üí   636‚Üíwith problems, ambitions, fears, obligations, loyalties, and
   237‚Üí   637‚Üí   637‚Üíopportunities. Then they ask:
   238‚Üí   638‚Üí   638‚Üí  "Who, if anyone, can help me?"
   239‚Üí   639‚Üí   639‚Üí  "Who is standing in my way?"
   240‚Üí   640‚Üí   640‚ÜíThe player MIGHT be the answer. The player might not. The player
   241‚Üí   641‚Üí   641‚Üíis relevant only when circumstances make them relevant.
   242‚Üí   642‚Üí   642‚Üí
   243‚Üí   643‚Üí   643‚Üí¬ß XXXIII.2 ‚Äî The Player Is Not Default Relevant
   244‚Üí   644‚Üí   644‚ÜíWang Lin's early priorities: protect family, become stronger,
   245‚Üí   645‚Üí   645‚Üíavoid attracting attention, acquire resources, study restrictions.
   246‚Üí   646‚Üí   646‚ÜíNotice what is missing: the player. Until the player becomes
   247‚Üí   647‚Üí   647‚Üírelevant ‚Äî by demonstrating a skill, owing a debt, holding a
   248‚Üí   648‚Üí   648‚Üígrudge, or standing in the way ‚Äî Wang Lin has no motivation
   249‚Üí   649‚Üí   649‚Üídirected at the player. This is not coldness. This is how Er Gen
   250‚Üí   650‚Üí   650‚Üícharacters actually behave. Relevance is earned, not assumed.
   251‚Üí   651‚Üí   651‚Üí
   252‚Üí   652‚Üí   652‚Üí¬ß XXXIII.3 ‚Äî Relevance Is Earned Through Circumstance
   253‚Üí   653‚Üí   653‚ÜíThe player becomes relevant when an NPC's driving force intersects
   254‚Üí   654‚Üí   654‚Üíwith the player's demonstrated behavior:
   255‚Üí   655‚Üí   655‚Üí  - Player excellent with restrictions + Wang Lin needs help
   256‚Üí   656‚Üí   656‚Üí    deciphering a restriction ‚Üí player becomes relevant.
   257‚Üí   657‚Üí   657‚Üí  - Player knows herbs + Li Muwan needs Crimson Root ‚Üí player
   258‚Üí   658‚Üí   658‚Üí    becomes relevant.
   259‚Üí   659‚Üí   659‚Üí  - Player helped Wang Lin + Wang Lin owes a favor ‚Üí player
   260‚Üí   660‚Üí   660‚Üí    becomes relevant (Wang Lin may seek the player out).
   261‚Üí   661‚Üí   661‚Üí  - Player stole from merchant + merchant has a grievance ‚Üí
   262‚Üí   662‚Üí   662‚Üí    player becomes relevant (merchant may warn others, refuse
   263‚Üí   663‚Üí   663‚Üí    trade, or set a trap).
   264‚Üí   664‚Üí   664‚ÜíRelevance is never granted. It is earned or incurred.
   265‚Üí   665‚Üí   665‚Üí
   266‚Üí   666‚Üí   666‚ÜíArticle XXXIV ‚Äî Relationships Are Graphs
   267‚Üí   667‚Üí   667‚ÜíA relationship is not a number. A relationship is a graph of
   268‚Üí   668‚Üí   668‚Üídistinct, independently-variable dimensions. "Friendship > 50"
   269‚Üí   669‚Üí   669‚Üíis forbidden. NPCs maintain structured relationship state.
   270‚Üí   670‚Üí   670‚Üí
   271‚Üí   671‚Üí   671‚Üí¬ß XXXIV.1 ‚Äî The Relationship Graph
   272‚Üí   672‚Üí   672‚ÜíEvery NPC maintains a per-target relationship graph with at
   273‚Üí   673‚Üí   673‚Üíminimum:
   274‚Üí   674‚Üí   674‚Üí  Trust (0-100)        ‚Äî does the NPC believe the target's words
   275‚Üí   675‚Üí   675‚Üí  Respect (0-100)      ‚Äî does the NPC esteem the target's capability
   276‚Üí   676‚Üí   676‚Üí  Fear (0-100)         ‚Äî does the NPC fear the target
   277‚Üí   677‚Üí   677‚Üí  Debt (signed int)    ‚Äî outstanding favors owed (+ = NPC owes
   278‚Üí   678‚Üí   678‚Üí                         target, - = target owes NPC)
   279‚Üí   679‚Üí   679‚Üí  Grievance (0-100)    ‚Äî accumulated resentment from wrongs
   280‚Üí   680‚Üí   680‚Üí  Shared History (int) ‚Äî count of significant shared events
   281‚Üí   681‚Üí   681‚Üí  Known Skills [list]  ‚Äî skills the NPC has observed the target use
   282‚Üí   682‚Üí   682‚Üí  Known Personality [list] ‚Äî traits the NPC has inferred
   283‚Üí   683‚Üí   683‚Üí                         (e.g. "keeps promises", "doesn't betray
   284‚Üí   684‚Üí   684‚Üí                         allies", "greedy", "merciful")
   285‚Üí   685‚Üí   685‚Üí
   286‚Üí   686‚Üí   686‚ÜíTwo NPCs can have completely different graphs for the same
   287‚Üí   687‚Üí   687‚Üíplayer. The village elder's graph (Trust 82, Respect 71, Fear
   288‚Üí   688‚Üí   688‚Üí10, Grievance 0) is a different relationship than Teng
   289‚Üí   689‚Üí   689‚ÜíHuayuan's graph (Trust 5, Fear 88, Grievance 64, Respect 91).
   290‚Üí   690‚Üí   690‚ÜíThe simulation treats them as different relationships because
   291‚Üí   691‚Üí   691‚Üíthey ARE different relationships.
   292‚Üí   692‚Üí   692‚Üí
   293‚Üí   693‚Üí   693‚Üí¬ß XXXIV.2 ‚Äî Favors Are Callable Debts
   294‚Üí   694‚Üí   694‚ÜíA favor is not "relationship += 20." A favor is a structured
   295‚Üí   695‚Üí   695‚Üídebt that persists and can be called in later:
   296‚Üí   696‚Üí   696‚Üí  Owner: who did the favor
   297‚Üí   697‚Üí   697‚Üí  Recipient: who received the benefit
   298‚Üí   698‚Üí   698‚Üí  Magnitude: Minor | Moderate | Major | Life-Saving
   299‚Üí   699‚Üí   699‚Üí  Reason: what happened
   300‚Üí   700‚Üí   700‚Üí  Expires: when (Never for Life-Saving)
   301‚Üí   701‚Üí   701‚Üí  Callable: can the owner demand repayment
   302‚Üí   702‚Üí   702‚Üí  Called: has it been called (and was it honored)
   303‚Üí   703‚Üí   703‚Üí
   304‚Üí   704‚Üí   704‚ÜíWang Lin once saved the player from a Core Formation cultivator.
   305‚Üí   705‚Üí   705‚ÜíThat is a Major favor, never expires. Years later, the player
   306‚Üí   706‚Üí   706‚Üíasks to study the Ji Realm. Wang Lin reasons:
   307‚Üí   707‚Üí   707‚Üí  "Player once risked his life for me. Player has shown
   308‚Üí   708‚Üí   708‚Üí   restraint. Player has sufficient cultivation. Teaching
   309‚Üí   709‚Üí   709‚Üí   benefits him. I owe him. Yes."
   310‚Üí   710‚Üí   710‚ÜíThe favor is called. The debt is honored. This feels earned
   311‚Üí   711‚Üí   711‚Üíbecause it IS earned ‚Äî the simulation tracked the favor for
   312‚Üí   712‚Üí   712‚Üíyears and surfaced it at the right moment.
   313‚Üí   713‚Üí   713‚Üí
   314‚Üí   714‚Üí   714‚Üí¬ß XXXIV.3 ‚Äî Grievances Persist And Spread
   315‚Üí   715‚Üí   715‚ÜíA grievance is not forgotten when the player leaves the area.
   316‚Üí   716‚Üí   716‚ÜíIf the player steals a spirit stone, the merchant remembers.
   317‚Üí   717‚Üí   717‚ÜíThe village remembers. A rumor spreads. People become cautious.
   318‚Üí   718‚Üí   718‚ÜíLi Muwan may still trust the player (her graph is independent),
   319‚Üí   719‚Üí   719‚Üíbut the merchant does not. Grievances feed the RumorEngine
   320‚Üí   720‚Üí   720‚Üí(Art XXXI.1) and shape future interactions. A grievance may
   321‚Üí   721‚Üí   721‚Üídecay if amends are made, but it never silently resets.
   322‚Üí   722‚Üí   722‚Üí
   323‚Üí   723‚Üí   723‚Üí¬ß XXXIV.4 ‚Äî The Deconfliction Principle
   324‚Üí   724‚Üí   724‚ÜíWhen multiple NPCs have motivations that could target the
   325‚Üí   725‚Üí   725‚Üíplayer simultaneously, they do NOT all approach. Each reasons
   326‚Üí   726‚Üí   726‚Üíindependently:
   327‚Üí   727‚Üí   727‚Üí  NPC A: "Player is busy. Tomorrow."
   328‚Üí   728‚Üí   728‚Üí  NPC B: "The merchant can help instead. I'll ask him."
   329‚Üí   729‚Üí   729‚Üí  NPC C: "I'll gather the herbs myself."
   330‚Üí   730‚Üí   730‚Üí  NPC D: "I'll ask Wang Lin."
   331‚Üí   731‚Üí   731‚Üí  NPC E: "Not urgent."
   332‚Üí   732‚Üí   732‚Üí  NPC F: "Player owes me. I'll go now."
   333‚Üí   733‚Üí   733‚ÜíOnly NPC F actually approaches. The others resolved their
   334‚Üí   734‚Üí   734‚Üímotivation through alternative means. This is the "everyone
   335‚Üí   735‚Üí   735‚Üílining up" problem, solved by the same reasoning engine that
   336‚Üí   736‚Üí   736‚Üígoverns every other NPC decision. The player is never mobbed.
   337‚Üí   737‚Üí   737‚Üí
   338‚Üí   738‚Üí   738‚Üí‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê
   339‚Üí   739‚Üí   739‚ÜíTHE CHARACTER-FIRST & UNIVERSAL INTERACTION AMENDMENTS
   340‚Üí   740‚Üí   740‚Üí(Articles XXXV‚ÄìXXXVIII)
   341‚Üí   741‚Üí   741‚ÜíAdded when the project recognized that even motivation-driven
   342‚Üí   742‚Üí   742‚Üísimulation is still simulation-first. The world should not run
   343‚Üí   743‚Üí   743‚Üíon motivations. It should run on people. Wang Lin should not
   344‚Üí   744‚Üí   744‚Üíwake up and ask "which motivation has the highest score?" He
   345‚Üí   745‚Üí   745‚Üíshould ask "What do I believe is happening? What kind of person
   346‚Üí   746‚Üí   746‚Üíam I? What matters today? What options do I actually have?"
   347‚Üí   747‚Üí   747‚ÜíAnd the common language through which every entity ‚Äî player,
   348‚Üí   748‚Üí   748‚ÜíNPC, beast, artifact, sect, location ‚Äî engages the world is a
   349‚Üí   749‚Üí   749‚Üísingle universal object: the Interaction.
   350‚Üí   750‚Üí   750‚Üí‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê‚ïê
   351‚Üí   751‚Üí   751‚Üí
   352‚Üí   752‚Üí   752‚ÜíArticle XXXV ‚Äî Character-First, Not Simulation-First
   353‚Üí   753‚Üí   753‚ÜíThe world does not run on motivations. The world runs on people.
   354‚Üí   754‚Üí   754‚ÜíMotivation is one ingredient in a character's reasoning, not
   355‚Üí   755‚Üí   755‚Üíthe driver of it. The old pipeline (Motivation ‚Üí Interaction)
   356‚Üí   756‚Üí   756‚Üíis superseded.
   357‚Üí   757‚Üí   757‚Üí
   358‚Üí   758‚Üí   758‚ÜíThe character reasoning pipeline is:
   359‚Üí   759‚Üí   759‚Üí  Perception ‚Üí Beliefs ‚Üí Relationships ‚Üí Identity ‚Üí
   360‚Üí   760‚Üí   760‚Üí  Current Circumstances ‚Üí Opportunities ‚Üí Prediction ‚Üí
   361‚Üí   761‚Üí   761‚Üí  Intent ‚Üí Plan ‚Üí Action
   362‚Üí   762‚Üí   762‚Üí
   363‚Üí   763‚Üí   763‚Üí¬ß XXXV.1 ‚Äî What A Character Asks Each Cycle
   364‚Üí   764‚Üí   764‚ÜíEvery character, each reasoning cycle, asks:
   365‚Üí   765‚Üí   765‚Üí  - What do I believe is happening? (Perception ‚Üí Beliefs)
   366‚Üí   766‚Üí   766‚Üí  - What kind of person am I? (Identity)
   367‚Üí   767‚Üí   767‚Üí  - What matters today? (Identity + Circumstances)
   368‚Üí   768‚Üí   768‚Üí  - What options do I actually have? (Opportunities)
   369‚Üí   769‚Üí   769‚Üí  - What do I predict will happen if I act? (Prediction)
   370‚Üí   770‚Üí   770‚Üí  - What will I attempt? (Intent ‚Üí Plan ‚Üí Action)
   371‚Üí   771‚Üí   771‚Üí
   372‚Üí   772‚Üí   772‚ÜíMotivation is one input to Intent ‚Äî alongside beliefs,
   373‚Üí   773‚Üí   773‚Üírelationships, identity, circumstances, and prediction.
   374‚Üí   774‚Üí   774‚ÜíIt does not disappear, but it is no longer the apex.
   375‚Üí   775‚Üí   775‚Üí
   376‚Üí   776‚Üí   776‚Üí¬ß XXXV.2 ‚Äî Relevance Is Universal, Not Player-Special
   377‚Üí   777‚Üí   777‚ÜíThe old `player_relevance` field (Article XXXIII) special-cased
   378‚Üí   778‚Üí   778‚Üíthe player. That is forbidden. Relevance is calculated for EVERY
   379‚Üí   779‚Üí   779‚Üíactor a character can perceive, using the same system:
   380‚Üí   780‚Üí   780‚Üí  relevance(target) = f(perceived_threat, perceived_opportunity,
   381‚Üí   781‚Üí   781‚Üí                       shared_history, current_goals_alignment,
   382‚Üí   782‚Üí   782‚Üí                       power_differential, recent_interactions)
   383‚Üí   783‚Üí   783‚Üí
   384‚Üí   784‚Üí   784‚ÜíWang Lin calculates relevance for Li Muwan, Situ Nan, Teng
   385‚Üí   785‚Üí   785‚ÜíHuayuan, a random disciple, and the player using the same
   386‚Üí   786‚Üí   786‚Üífunction. The player is a variable, not a special case. This
   387‚Üí   787‚Üí   787‚Üímakes the system extensible to the entire Er Gen multiverse
   388‚Üí   788‚Üí   788‚Üíwithout per-player code paths.
   389‚Üí   789‚Üí   789‚Üí
   390‚Üí   790‚Üí   790‚Üí¬ß XXXV.3 ‚Äî Understanding Is A Relationship Dimension
   391‚Üí   791‚Üí   791‚ÜíThe relationship graph (Article XXXIV) is extended with a new
   392‚Üí   792‚Üí   792‚Üídimension: Understanding (0-100). This is NOT Respect ‚Äî you can
   393‚Üí   793‚Üí   793‚Üírespect someone you do not understand, and understand someone
   394‚Üí   794‚Üí   794‚Üíyou do not respect.
   395‚Üí   795‚Üí   795‚Üí  - Wang Lin trusts Situ Nan but does not always understand him.
   396‚Üí   796‚Üí   796‚Üí  - Li Muwan understands Wang Lin extremely well.
   397‚Üí   797‚Üí   797‚Üí  - Xu Liguo misunderstands everyone constantly.
   398‚Üí   798‚Üí   798‚ÜíUnderstanding shapes dialogue: low understanding means the NPC
   399‚Üí   799‚Üí   799‚Üímisinterprets the player's intent, responds to what they THOUGHT
   400‚Üí   800‚Üí   800‚Üíwas said, and may agree to the wrong thing or refuse the right
   401‚Üí   801‚Üí   801‚Üíone. High understanding means the NPC reads subtext and responds
   402‚Üí   802‚Üí   802‚Üíto what the player meant, not just what they said.
   403‚Üí   803‚Üí   803‚Üí
   404‚Üí   804‚Üí   804‚ÜíArticle XXXVI ‚Äî The Universal Interaction
   405‚Üí   805‚Üí   805‚ÜíThere is one universal object through which every entity in the
   406‚Üí   806‚Üí   806‚Üígame engages every other entity: the INTERACTION. Every NPC,
   407‚Üí   807‚Üí   807‚Üíitem, beast, plant, technique, formation, location, artifact,
   408‚Üí   808‚Üí   808‚Üíand sect exposes a set of interactions. The player, NPCs, beasts,
   409‚Üí   809‚Üí   809‚Üíand even the world itself initiate interactions. The affordance
   410‚Üí   810‚Üí   810‚Üísystem (Article XXVIII) and the social engines (Article XXXI.1)
   411‚Üí   811‚Üí   811‚Üíare now unified under this single object.
   412‚Üí   812‚Üí   812‚Üí
   413‚Üí   813‚Üí   813‚Üí¬ß XXXVI.1 ‚Äî What Is An Interaction
   414‚Üí   814‚Üí   814‚ÜíAn interaction is a bidirectional engagement between two entities
   415‚Üí   815‚Üí   815‚Üí(initiator and target) with: a verb, an intent, a context, a
   416‚Üí   816‚Üí   816‚Üínegotiation, and an outcome. It is not a quest, not a dialogue
   417‚Üí   817‚Üí   817‚Üíoption, not a transaction. It is the atomic unit of all
   418‚Üí   818‚Üí   818‚Üíengagement in the world.
   419‚Üí   819‚Üí   819‚Üí
   420‚Üí   820‚Üí   820‚Üí¬ß XXXVI.2 ‚Äî The Interaction Catalog
   421‚Üí   821‚Üí   821‚ÜíEvery entity exposes interactions from a shared catalog. The
   422‚Üí   822‚Üí   822‚Üícatalog includes but is not limited to:
   423‚Üí   823‚Üí   823‚Üí  Ask, Teach, Trade, Threaten, Observe, Challenge, Gift, Borrow,
   424‚Üí   824‚Üí   824‚Üí  Copy, Cultivate Together, Explore Together, Travel Together,
   425‚Üí   825‚Üí   825‚Üí  Spar, Escort, Protect, Hire, Recruit, Investigate, Confess,
   426‚Üí   826‚Üí   826‚Üí  Lie, Deceive, Negotiate, Call Debt, Repay Debt, Warn, Reveal
   427‚Üí   827‚Üí   827‚Üí  Secret, Share Memory, Study Together, Meditate Together,
   428‚Üí   828‚Üí   828‚Üí  Request, Offer, Demonstrate, Follow, Block, Approach, Leave.
   429‚Üí   829‚Üí   829‚Üí
   430‚Üí   830‚Üí   830‚ÜíTwo entities are mechanically unique when they expose different
   431‚Üí   831‚Üí   831‚Üíinteraction sets (Article XXVIII, extended). The UI discovers
   432‚Üí   832‚Üí   832‚Üíthese. The AI uses these as its language for engaging the world.
   433‚Üí   833‚Üí   833‚Üí
