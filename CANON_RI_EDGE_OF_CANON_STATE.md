# Part 1 — The Core Truth at the Edge of Canon

## 1.1 What Has Changed Cosmologically

The Cave World is no longer a farm. Wang Lin killed the Seven-Colored Daoist (E30, ~Year 2000), became the new owner, renamed it "Wang Lin's Cave World," and dissolved the Realm-Sealing Grand Array. The cultivation ceiling is lifted. Third-Step cultivators can now arise naturally inside the former Sealed Realm. The Sealed Realm and Outer Realm are unified — there is no longer an array separating them.

The Joss Flame harvest still flows. Wang Lin, as the new owner, receives it. But Wang Lin has Transcended. He does not actively farm these flames. The harvest continues mechanically, accumulating. Whether the player can tap into this flow is an open question — the owner's authority is still Wang Lin's, even in absence.

The Heaven-Defying Bead is gone from the world. It fused with Wang Lin's primordial spirit and left with him. The bead that Wang Lin found at age 16 inside the Heng Yue Sect's dead bird is no longer findable. No future cultivator will receive Situ Nan's transmission from that bead. Situ Nan's Underworld Ascension Method, his trio of incomplete celestial finger spells, the Vermilion Bird Burning Heaven Art — these are no longer transmitted through that channel.

The Heaven-Splitting Axe's status is ambiguous. It was the spirit of the Realm-Sealing Grand Array. When Wang Lin dissolved the array, the Axe was freed. Per the timeline, Wang Lin erected a replacement formation (E70, the Unnamed Wheel Formation, Ch. 1667) and the Axe cooperated with him before the array's dissolution. Whether the Axe is dormant, freed, departed, or still present is not explicitly stated. This is a C3 (novel-implicit) gap — the Axe may still exist in the world as a free entity.

## 1.3 The Decision Horizon Principle

The user's directive: NPCs think on different timescales. This is not a game-mechanic abstraction — it is a canon-derived truth about how cultivation shapes cognition.

- **Mortal NPCs** (no cultivation): Decision horizon = days to years. They think about harvests, marriages, debts, local politics. A village elder plans for his grandchildren.
- **Foundation-to-Core NPCs**: Decision horizon = decades. A Core Formation elder thinks in terms of sect succession, resource accumulation over centuries, regional dominance.
- **Nascent-to-Soul-Formation NPCs**: Decision horizon = centuries. They plan generational schemes. The All-Seer's possession plot spanned Wang Lin's entire mortal-to-Ascendant journey (~300 years). That was a SHORT plan by Soul Formation standards.
- **Nirvana-to-Ascendant NPCs**: Decision horizon = millennia. They wait for cosmic alignments, reincarnation cycles, inheritance ripening. Bai Fan's inheritance sat for ~10,000 years. Tu Si's legacy sat for ~50,000 years.
- **Third-Step NPCs**: Decision horizon = tens of millennia. The Seven-Colored Daoist maintained a farm for ~100,000 years. He did not act hastily. He treated cultivators as livestock, not enemies.
- **Transcendent NPCs (Wang Lin)**: Decision horizon = beyond time. Wang Lin exists outside local Allheaven law. His manifestation returns to the lower worlds on a schedule that mortals cannot perceive.

# Part 3 — Inheritance Accessibility Matrix

> The user's directive: "Even if things are unique to certain NPCs, I still want to be able to have access to them. I'm a protagonist, so there's gotta be a way."
>
> This classifies every major inheritance not by whether the player CAN get it, but by WHAT CONDITIONS must be met. Some are teachable. Some are location-locked. Some require recognition. Some can be recreated. The player is the protagonist — there is always a path. But the path may be long, dangerous, or require creativity.

## IA-01. The Underworld Ascension Method (Ji Realm cultivation)
- **Canon owner:** Situ Nan (2nd-Gen Vermilion Bird)
- **Current owner:** Gone — Situ Nan is on the IAC with restored memories; the Heaven-Defying Bead (which contained the teaching chamber) is with Wang Lin
- **Can it be duplicated?** YES — Situ Nan transmitted it verbally to Wang Lin. It is a knowledge-based technique. If any cultivator learned it from Situ Nan before his departure, or if written records exist, it can be transmitted again.
- **Can it be taught?** YES — Situ Nan taught it to Wang Lin inside the bead. Any Ji Realm cultivator who knows it can teach it.
- **Does learning it weaken the teacher?** NO — verbal transmission does not weaken the transmitter.
- **Does it require recognition?** NO — but Situ Nan chose to teach Wang Lin because of the bead connection.
- **Does it disappear after inheritance?** NO.
- **Tied to karma?** Loosely — the technique is associated with Situ Nan's karma. Using it may attract Situ Nan's attention (he is alive on the IAC).
- **Tied to bloodline?** NO.
- **Tied to a location?** The teaching happened inside the Heaven-Defying Bead (now gone). But the technique itself is portable.
- **Can it be recreated?** NO — it is a specific cultivation method created by a specific cultivator.
- **Player access path:** The technique was taught verbally. If the player can find a cultivator who learned it from Situ Nan's lineage (Heng Yue Sect elders who knew Huang Long Zhenren/Lu Yun), or find written records in the Vermilion Bird Divine Sect's archives, they can learn it. The Ji Realm itself is a divine-sense transformation triggered by extreme trauma + the method — the player would need both the method AND the catalyst. Alternatively, the player could seek out Situ Nan on the IAC (he is alive as "Si Nan") and request direct transmission. As the protagonist, a path exists — but it requires reaching the IAC and earning Situ Nan's recognition.

## IA-02. The Ancient God Tactic (body cultivation)
- **Canon owner:** Tu Si (8-Star Ancient God, long dead)
- **Current owner:** Tu Si's body IS the Land of the Ancient God. The tactic is embedded in the body's memory legacy.
- **Can it be duplicated?** PARTIALLY — the essence ("plunder everything") is a philosophy, not a secret formula. Any cultivator who enters Tu Si's body and survives the trial can inherit it.
- **Can it be taught?** ONLY via the trial — the full tactic requires the memory legacy inside Tu Si's body. A partial version could be taught by someone who already inherited it (Wang Lin, Tuo Sen), but the full version requires the trial.
- **Does learning it weaken the teacher?** NO — the trial is in the body, not from a living teacher.
- **Does it require recognition?** YES — the memory legacy judges the inheritor. Tu Si chose Wang Lin.

# Part 5 — The Universe as the Threat

> The user's directive: "The player doesn't need a replacement Teng Huayuan. The universe is the threat."

### The Ceiling Is Gone
For 100,000 years, no cultivator inside the Cave World could exceed Nirvana Fruit (Heaven Blight could squeeze through). Now the ceiling is lifted. ### The Joss Flame Accumulation
The Joss Flame harvest has been flowing to Wang Lin (now absent) since he killed the Seven-Colored Daoist. Decades or centuries of uncollected Joss Flames are accumulating in the world's spiritual layer. This is an enormous amount of cultivation energy with no outlet. What happens when it reaches a critical mass? No one knows — this has never happened before. The accumulation could: (1) spontaneously trigger spiritual catastrophes, (2) create new spirit veins, (3) attract entities that feed on Joss Flames, (4) cause the world's spiritual density to increase permanently, making cultivation easier for everyone but also making the world more unstable.

### The Power Vacuum Cascade
Wang Lin removed: the Seven-Colored Daoist, the All-Seer, Teng Huayuan, the Blood Ancestor, Daoist Water, Gu Dao, the Dao Devil Sect Master, and multiple other powers. Each removal created a vacuum. Nature fills vacuums. Smaller sects expand into former Teng Clan territory. Bandits appear in the disrupted regions. Ancient beasts reclaim territory from destroyed sects. New alliances form among survivors. Merchant routes change. The player is entering a world in the middle of a massive political and ecological reshuffling — not because a villain caused it, but because the strongest being in the world's history removed every obstacle in his path and then left.

### The Karma of Wang Lin's Slaughter
Wang Lin killed millions. The 18 Layers of Hell inside his Celestial Sealing Stamp stores all those souls. The Karma Whip cleaved open 7 million worlds. The Slaughter Essence was completed by consuming Qing Shui's Black Sword. This karmic weight does not disappear because Wang Lin left. It lingers in the world. These stains attract karmic entities, create spiritual distortions, and can affect cultivators who pass through them. A player who walks through the Teng Clan ruins may experience the echoes of Wang Lin's slaughter — not as a ghost story, but as a karmic disturbance that affects their cultivation, their Dao heart, and their perception.

### Surviving High-Tier Cultivators
Not every powerful being is dead. The 8 surviving Grand Empyreans on the IAC are not villains, but they are EXTREMELY powerful cultivators with their own agendas. They existed before Wang Lin, they survived Wang Lin, and they will exist after the player. They are not "replacement antagonists" — they are the universe's established powers. If the player threatens their interests, they will act. Not because the player is "the chosen one," but because the player has entered their awareness sphere and become relevant to their goals.

### The Heaven-Splitting Axe (if still present)
If the Axe was freed when the array was dissolved and not destroyed, it is now a free sentient artifact of immense power. Its motivations are unknown. It may be dormant, it may be wandering, it may be seeking a new purpose. A free sentient artifact of the Heaven-Splitting Axe's tier is not a villain — it is a force of nature that the player may encounter.

## 7.1 What This Means for the Player

The player enters a world where:
1. **The strongest being who ever lived here is still alive** — just absent. His shadow is everywhere. His actions shaped everything. But he is not directing the world from afar. The world is proceeding without him.
2. **The antagonists are dead.** The All-Seer, the Seven-Colored Daoist, Teng Huayuan, the Blood Ancestor, Daoist Water — all killed by Wang Lin. The player does not need to fight them.
3. **The universe itself is the challenge.** The ceiling is gone. The power vacuums are filled by ambitious cultivators. The Joss Flames accumulate. The karmic stains linger. New cultivators are born, grow, scheme, and die in a world permanently altered by one man's 2,000-year journey.
4. **Wang Lin's left-behind resources are tools, not a script.** The player can learn what Wang Lin learned, visit where Wang Lin visited, and walk a similar path — but the player is forging their OWN journey, not retelling Wang Lin's.
5. **The player is the protagonist.** Unique inheritances have paths. The Heaven-Defying Bead is gone, but everything else has a way. The player can learn everything Wang Lin learned and more — because the player is the protagonist of THIS story, not a spectator of Wang Lin's.
